# Top 15 Shortlist — Operator Email Round

## What this is

15 specific buildings across 14 distinct operators that survived a multi-stage filter+research pipeline. Each entry below carries everything needed to send a tailored email today: verified qualification mechanics, the lead question that resolves the load-bearing unknown, direct contact details, anchor pricing, and a justification for the rank.

## How to use this

1. **Send all 15 emails this week.** Each operator gets a personalised first message with the lead question for that operator. Do not blanket-template — the question that matters is different for each.
2. **Lead with savings + parental support framing.** Caner has zero UK income but his parents are self-employed lawyers in Turkey. Frame the financial picture as: *"Open Banking visibility of UK savings + monthly family transfers + Graduate visa to Aug 2027 + willingness to use Guarantid/Housing Hand professional guarantor"*.
3. **Verify pricing yourself.** Every price below is from agent research on 2026-04-16. Caner can cross-check on operator websites in 5 min — pricing is the field that decays fastest.
4. **Treat ranks 1-12 as primary; 13-15 as bridge insurance.** If 12 of these come back negative, ranks 13-15 give you a 30-day forced-relocation landing pad.
5. **The RRA commences 1 May 2026.** If anything below references a "pay 6-12 months upfront" workaround, that's pre-RRA practice and is now capped at 1 month for ASTs. Licence-based operators (co-living, apart-hotels) are exempt and remain flexible on advance rent.

## Scoring framework

Each candidate scored on two-tier composite (per cross-agent guidance):

**ACCESS dimensions (gates the candidate):**
- `referencing_pathway` — strong / medium / weak (×3)
- `budget_headroom` — comfortable / tight / over (×2)
- `evidence_confidence` — high / medium / low (×1)

**LONG-STAY dimensions (tiebreaker among accessible candidates):**
- `long_stay_viable` — yes / partial / no (×3) — driven by joint condition (RRA-periodic + guarantor pathway works) OR licence-with-multi-year-history
- `unit_quality` — good / ok / cramped (×2)
- `location_fit` — strong / acceptable / weak vs Caner's weekly pattern (café work, gym, social, Z1-3 connectivity) (×1)

Bridge-only candidates (apart-hotels) automatically score 0 on long-stay dimensions and are flagged BRIDGE.

## Cross-cutting observations from the research round

1. **Homeppl is the structural unlock for Caner's profile across BTR.** Get Living, Quintain Living, Greystar (Pearl Yard / Bloom / Mylo), Fizzy Living, and Morro all confirmed Homeppl. Open Banking + thin-credit-file friendliness + Guarantid integration = the closest thing to a default pathway in modern BTR.

2. **The RRA fundamentally reshaped the qualification landscape.** Every "pay 6-12 months upfront for international applicants" historical workaround is dead from 1 May 2026. Operators who relied on this (Folio London, Essential Living per their own blog, Way of Life, Quintain's documented fallback) now need to reroute Caner's profile through guarantor services. **Mention RRA explicitly in every email** — it creates leverage to ask "given the upfront route is gone, what's your current pathway for non-UK-income applicants?"

3. **Licence-based operators (co-living, apart-hotels) retain advance-rent flexibility.** Folk, Node Living, ARK, Blueground, The Gate, Dandi all operate licences not ASTs, so the RRA 1-month cap doesn't bind. This makes them strategically advantaged for Caner's profile — multi-month upfront payment remains a viable substitute for income evidence.

4. **The "BTR operator says we accept guarantors" wording does NOT automatically include professional services.** Grainger's wording is broad ("we do accept guarantors"). Way of Life's is narrow ("UK-based guarantor"). The interpretation risk on professional services like Housing Hand is real and varies operator-by-operator. Always ask about Housing Hand AND Guarantid by name in the lead enquiry.

5. **Long-stay rent escalation is the under-discussed risk for Caner's 2-3 year intent.** Quintain Living has documented 25-33% rent hikes at renewal across multiple HomeViews/Trustpilot accounts. ARK and other licence-based operators reprice at each membership renewal. Section 13 challenges under RRA are available but practically mean fighting yearly. **Budget defensively for years 2-3 at +20-30% rent** at Quintain specifically.

6. **Caner's existing Greystar tenant history is a real asset.** Pearl Yard / Bermondsey Project / Bloom Nine Elms / Mylo Nine Elms are all Greystar direct-operated. His clean rental record from Ten Degrees (also Greystar) is a behavioural prior the operator can credit, even though it's not a formal transferable reference. Lead with this in the Greystar email.

7. **Three operators in this shortlist are NOT in the Flatbrowser dataset and should be added:**
   - **Blueground** — multi-area portfolio, possibly needs new schema pattern (operator that isn't building-specific)
   - **Roomzzz Stratford** — add to `stratford.ts`
   - **Adagio Brentford** — add to `kew-bridge-brentford.ts`

8. **The "3-month minimum" is a meaningful differentiator.** Pearl Yard (Greystar), Folk, Node Living, ARK, Quintain Living, Vonder, Bravo Quarters, Gravity Co all offer 3-month or shorter minimums. Means Caner can sign without 12-month commitment risk if his situation evolves (e.g. lands a job and wants to upgrade).
