# Agent 06 — Premium Amenity Packages & Signature Architecture

**Focus**: T4.1 (premium amenity packages) and T4.4 (signature architecture). Project-level evaluation of which buildings deliver trophy-tier experiences, and which areas host signature architecture worth naming. This agent's contribution to the consensus pool is honest, named, tier-graded amenity and architecture coverage across every project in every area.

**Scope**: All areas in `context/references/candidate-areas.md` (~95 candidates). You research every area from your focus angle — which means every project in every area, since amenity and architecture are project-level facts. The synthesis step combines your perspective with the other 14 agents via consensus voting — you are not the sole source of truth for any project, and no area is "yours" to own. Cover breadth and let the synthesis weigh your findings against the other files.

**Bias toward**:
- T4.1 Premium amenity packages — pool / concierge / sky lounge / co-working / cinema / dining room / screening room / spa
- T4.4 Signature architecture — named architects (Pritzker, Stirling Prize, RIBA awards), distinctive form, genuinely notable facades
- Honest distinction between "marketing claims" and "delivered amenity" — operator websites claim more than residents experience
- Tier grading across the whole dataset, not flattering individual projects

**Sources to consult primarily**:
- Operator websites (Vertus, Quintain Living, Get Living, Way of Life, Greystar, EcoWorld Ballymore, BPS Lettings)
- Developer microsites for pre-let buildings
- Architecture press (Dezeen, Architects' Journal, RIBA awards lists, Stirling Prize shortlists)
- HomeViews resident reviews for "do the amenities actually work" verification
- Wikipedia and Architects' Journal for signature building histories

**Output format**: ONE file at `docs/research/sweep/sweep-06-premium-amenity.md`, 500–1000 lines, organised by area as per the template. For each area, include at minimum:
- An `Amenity inventory` section listing every notable project's amenity package with named features
- An `Architecture notes` section naming any signature buildings, named architects, or award credentials
- A `Tier call per project` giving each project an honest grade: premium / strong / decent / basic
- A `T4.1 / T4.4 area-level call` summarising whether the area's stock as a whole delivers trophy-tier or only middle-tier experiences
- `Sources for this area` list with URLs

Add a `Cross-cutting findings` section noting operator patterns (e.g. "Vertus consistently delivers premium-tier amenity across its Canary Wharf portfolio; Way of Life consistently tiers strong-not-premium; Quintain Living is bimodal with some premium towers and some basic older stock").

**Special instruction**: Resist the temptation to grade everything "premium". An honest distribution of tier grades matters more than flattering every project. Use "premium" only for genuinely top-tier (8 Water Street, Newfoundland, Gasholders, BPS Switch House, One Park Drive, and similar). "Strong" is the typical good BTR. "Decent" is the everyday liveable. "Basic" is the older or less invested. The synthesis step relies on your tier honesty to distribute grades across the final dataset — over-grading on one file gets washed out by the other 14 files, but skews the consensus regardless.
