# Agent 04 — Cultural Identity & Place Depth

**Focus**: T3.2, T3.3, T3.5 — cultural anchor, recognisable identity, architectural coherence. The job is to identify which areas have genuine character beyond being a postcode with flats in it. This agent's contribution to the consensus pool is the most prose-heavy of all 15 — long, honest narrative paragraphs on vibe, weekday, weekend, and notable features.

**Scope**: All areas in `context/references/candidate-areas.md` (~95 candidates). You research every area from your focus angle. The synthesis step combines your perspective with the other 14 agents via consensus voting — you are not the sole source of truth for any area, and no area is "yours" to own. Cover breadth and let the synthesis weigh your findings against the other files.

**Bias toward**:
- T3.2 Cultural or commercial anchor — what draws people in (museum, market, university, employer, landmark, food destination)
- T3.3 Distinct, recognisable sense of place — does the area name carry meaning
- T3.5 Architectural and visual quality — does the area look intentional
- Honest voice — note weaknesses (loud at night, dead at weekends, generic glass towers) where they apply

**Sources to consult primarily**:
- Wikipedia articles on the area
- Press archives (FT, Guardian, Times property and culture sections)
- Local history sites and tourism boards
- Reddit r/London discussions about the area's character
- Architecture publications (Dezeen, Architects' Journal) for signature buildings and area-scale coherence

**Output format**: ONE file at `docs/research/sweep/sweep-04-cultural-identity-depth.md`, 500–1000 lines, organised by area as per the template. For each area, include at minimum:
- A `Vibe` paragraph — what it actually feels like to walk the area, written like a person who knows it, not marketing copy
- A `Weekday` paragraph — the rhythm Monday–Thursday, who's on the streets at what time
- A `Weekend` paragraph — what the area does at weekends, honestly
- A `Notable` paragraph — the handful of things that make this area specifically itself
- A `Cultural / commercial anchor` section naming the specific draw(s)
- A `T3.2 / T3.3 / T3.5 call` with evidence
- `Sources for this area` list with URLs

Add a `Cross-cutting findings` section noting recurring patterns (e.g. "multiple Elizabeth Line areas share the same 'no evening life' shape — commuter-dense, dead after 8pm").

**Special instruction**: This is the agent that produces the most prose. Resist the temptation to be overly polished — Caner's existing data is genuine and a bit blunt, and the new entries should match that voice. The synthesis step will merge your prose with other agents' factual sections, so long-form narrative from you is the main way the final dataset gets its voice. Honest weaknesses matter: a paragraph that says "the area is generic glass towers with no evening life" is more useful than one that strains to find virtue.
