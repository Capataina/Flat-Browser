# Agent 04 — Cultural Identity & Place Depth

**Focus**: T3.2, T3.3, T3.5 — cultural anchor, recognisable identity, architectural coherence. The job is to identify which areas have genuine character beyond being a postcode with flats in it.

**Primary scope**: Whitechapel, Aldgate East, Hackney Wick, Dalston, Highbury, Angel/Islington, Camden Town, Notting Hill, South Kensington, Marylebone, Bermondsey, Borough/London Bridge, Brixton, Peckham, Crystal Palace, Pimlico, Hammersmith, Wapping

**Secondary scope**: Fill `area.long_form` (especially `vibe`, `weekday`, `weekend`, `notable`) for every area in the candidate list. Also fill T3.2/T3.3/T3.5 evaluations.

**Bias toward**:
- T3.2 Cultural or commercial anchor — what draws people in (museum, market, university, employer, landmark, food destination)
- T3.3 Distinct, recognisable sense of place — does the area name carry meaning
- T3.5 Architectural and visual quality — does the area look intentional

**Sources to consult primarily**:
- Wikipedia articles on the area
- Press archives (FT, Guardian, Times property and culture sections)
- Local history sites and tourism boards
- Reddit r/London discussions about the area's character
- Architecture publications (Dezeen, Architects' Journal) for signature buildings

**Output focus**:
- Long, honest narrative `vibe`, `weekday`, `weekend`, `notable` paragraphs that read like a person who knows the area, not marketing copy
- T3 tier evaluation with specific anchor names and reasoning
- Identify any cultural anchors that haven't been documented in existing entries

**Special instruction**: This is the agent that produces the most prose. Resist the temptation to be overly polished — Caner's existing data is genuine and a bit blunt, and the new entries should match that voice. Note honest weaknesses (loud at night, dead at weekends, generic glass towers) where they apply.
