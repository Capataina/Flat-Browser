# Agent 01 — Safety & Foundational Viability

**Focus**: Tier 1 disqualification and foundational viability. This agent is the gatekeeper lens — its contribution to the consensus pool is whether each area passes T1 cleanly, and if not, where it fails. Other agents will independently touch safety too; this agent's job is to make its angle on T1 as dense and well-sourced as any single file in the sweep.

**Scope**: All areas in `context/references/candidate-areas.md` (~95 candidates). You research every area from your focus angle. The synthesis step combines your perspective with the other 14 agents via consensus voting — you are not the sole source of truth for any area, and no area is "yours" to own. Cover breadth and let the synthesis weigh your findings against the other files.

**Bias toward**:
- T1.1 Safer than Croydon — Met Police crime data per ward, overall borough rankings, after-dark feel
- T1.2 Modern rental stock exists at all — at least one viable BTR/managed development
- T1.3 Strong connectivity to central London — you touch this lightly; agent 7 is the deep connectivity angle
- T1.4 Not a known anti-social hotspot — local press, council ASB reports, Reddit sentiment
- T1.5 Public realm in good order — Street View walks, recent council investment, vacancy rates
- T1.6 Not actively in decline — high street vacancy, employer departures, population trends

**Sources to consult primarily**:
- Met Police crime data dashboard (https://www.met.police.uk/sd/stats-and-data/met/crime-data-dashboard/)
- ONS borough crime statistics
- Recent press / Reddit threads on safety (cross-check anecdotal vs statistical)
- London council websites for public realm investment announcements
- Google Street View for visual confirmation of public realm condition

**Output format**: ONE file at `docs/research/sweep-01-safety-foundational.md`, 500–1000 lines, organised by area as per the template. For each area, include at minimum:
- A `Safety` section covering crime data, after-dark assessment, comparison vs Croydon, and any recent trend
- A `T1 pass/fail call` section stating whether the area passes each of T1.1–T1.6 and the reasoning
- `Sources for this area` list with URLs
- A confidence tag ("high" / "medium" / "low") against your own research for that area

Add a `Cross-cutting findings` section noting borough-level patterns (e.g. "all three Hackney candidates sit within the top quintile for ASB but rank safer than Croydon on violent crime").

**Special instruction**: If an area fails T1.1 (safer than Croydon) cleanly, say so explicitly in your area section with the evidence. The synthesis step will weight your dissent against the other 14 files, but a clear fail with strong sourcing is the single most consequential contribution this agent can make — a soft "moderate concerns" is less useful than a concrete "Met Police data places ward X in the worst quintile for violent crime, directly comparable to Croydon's worst wards".
