# Agent 08 — Regeneration & 2027 Trajectory

**Focus**: T3.1 (active regeneration / current investment) and T5.3 (long-term ascending trajectory through 2027 — Caner's visa transition window). This agent's contribution to the consensus pool is forward-looking: which areas are still ascending into August 2027, which are peaking before, and which have already plateaued.

**Scope**: All areas in `context/references/candidate-areas.md` (~95 candidates). You research every area from your focus angle. The synthesis step combines your perspective with the other 14 agents via consensus voting — you are not the sole source of truth for any area, and no area is "yours" to own. Cover breadth and let the synthesis weigh your findings against the other files.

**Bias toward**:
- T3.1 Active regeneration — visible ongoing investment, masterplan still being delivered, cranes still up in 2026
- T5.3 Long-term ascending trajectory — investment pipeline announcements, planning permissions, where the area will be in August 2027
- Honest classification of peak timing — "already peaked" vs "peaking 2025–2026" vs "ascending into 2027–2028+"

**Sources to consult primarily**:
- GLA London Development Database
- Borough council planning portals (each borough has its own)
- Press archives for planning permissions and development announcements
- Developer portfolio pages (Berkeley, Argent, Lendlease, Quintain, British Land, Related, Knight Dragon, Ballymore)
- New London Architecture (NLA) for major project tracking

**Output format**: ONE file at `docs/research/sweep/sweep-08-regeneration-2027-trajectory.md`, 500–1000 lines, organised by area as per the template. For each area, include at minimum:
- A `Current status` section describing where the masterplan (if any) stands today — phases complete, phases under construction, phases consented
- A `Pipeline` section listing approved or announced projects with dates and scale
- A `Trajectory through 2027` paragraph describing where the area will be in August 2027 specifically — this is unusual but deliberate, see special instruction
- A `T3.1 / T5.3 call` with specific evidence and a confidence tag
- `Sources for this area` list with URLs

Add a `Cross-cutting findings` section noting patterns (e.g. "the Opportunity Areas designated in the London Plan are consistently 3–5 years behind schedule" or "every area with Berkeley masterplan involvement is ascending through 2027").

**Special instruction**: The 2027 trajectory paragraph is unusual but deliberate — Caner's visa expires in August 2027, so an area that peaks before then is a worse long-term bet than an area still ascending across that horizon. Use this lens explicitly for every area, not just the obvious ones. Areas like Brent Cross Town, Tottenham Hale, and Old Oak Common are obvious "ascending into 2027+" candidates, but there are less obvious ones too — any area with a masterplan phase completing in 2027 deserves the same lens. The synthesis step will weight your trajectory calls against other agents' vibe and amenity calls to produce the final T5.3 assessment.
