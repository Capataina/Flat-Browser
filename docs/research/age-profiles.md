# Age Profile Research Notes

These age tags are estimated, not directly measured resident averages. Most London developments do not publish resident age distributions, so the tags are inferred from three inputs:

1. Borough or local-area demographic profiles.
2. The way each development is positioned and programmed by its operator.
3. The difference between resident mix and visitor/through-traffic mix for destination districts.

The tags in the product are therefore best read as likely dominant age bands, not as exact census outputs for each scheme.

## Place-by-Place Notes

### Wembley Park

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Brent is a comparatively young borough, and Wembley Park operates as a large rental-led leisure district with student-facing marketing, events, retail and a strong early-career renter profile. Even so, the dominant resident centre of gravity still looks more like established young professionals and early family households than a mainly under-30 enclave.
- Sources: [Brent borough profile](https://www.brent.gov.uk/the-council-and-democracy/sharing-our-information/brent-data-hub/borough-profile/people), [Wembley Park living](https://www.wembleypark.com/living/)

### Greenwich Peninsula

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Greenwich is relatively young by London standards, and the Peninsula is programmed around the O2, Design District, student-adjacent creative uses and newer rental stock. That keeps the footfall broad and youthful, but the dominant resident and repeat-user profile still reads more as 30-40 than under-30.
- Sources: [Royal Greenwich facts and figures](https://www.royalgreenwich.gov.uk/info/200245/facts_and_figures_about_greenwich), [Greenwich Peninsula](https://www.greenwichpeninsula.co.uk/about/)

### Stratford / East Bank

- Product tag: `18-30`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Stratford has one of the clearest younger-adult signals in the set: major rail interchange, university and culture anchors, Olympic Park employment, and a local profile with substantial young adult density. Footfall spans all ages, but the active age profile skews younger here than in the premium central ownership districts.
- Sources: [East Bank](https://www.eastbank.org.uk), [Stratford and New Town local age profile](https://censusdata.uk/e09000025-stratford-and-new-town-e05000623)

### Battersea Power Station

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Wandsworth has strong working-age representation, but Battersea Power Station itself sits at the premium end of the market. Resident age is pulled upward by pricing and ownership options, while visitor flow remains unusually broad because it is also a London destination. The dominant band still looks more 30-40 than 40+ because the ultra-prime older skew is concentrated in specific sub-addresses like Switch House.
- Sources: [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf), [Battersea Power Station homes](https://batterseapowerstation.co.uk/live/homes/)

### King's Cross

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Camden has a strong younger adult population, and King's Cross combines higher-end housing with universities, offices, stations and cultural space. The daytime crowd is broad and younger than the resident base, but the dominant live-work-spend profile still feels more 30-40 than 18-30.
- Sources: [Camden population and demography](https://www.camden.gov.uk/population-and-demography), [King's Cross live, work, study](https://www.kingscross.co.uk/live-work-study)

### Canary Wharf / Wood Wharf

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Tower Hamlets is one of London's younger boroughs, and Wood Wharf is built around professional renters, office workers and newer apartment stock. The blend is broad, but the centre of gravity reads more as established working-age adults in their thirties than either students or older owners.
- Sources: [Tower Hamlets borough profile](https://www.towerhamlets.gov.uk/lgnl/council_and_democracy/data_and_statistics/Borough-profile.aspx), [Wood Wharf](https://group.canarywharf.com/portfolio/wood-wharf/)

### Elephant Park

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Southwark has a young adult-heavy demographic profile, and Elephant Park's central location, new-build stock, and mixed rental/ownership positioning make it attractive to younger professionals and early family households rather than an older luxury-owner audience. The dominant band still looks closer to 30-40 than 18-30.
- Sources: [Southwark JSNA demographics](https://www.southwark.gov.uk/health-and-wellbeing/public-health/insight-and-data/JSNA/demographics), [Elephant Park](https://elephantpark.co.uk)

### Royal Wharf

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Newham is young overall, and Royal Wharf reads as a mass-market, professionally managed riverside district that attracts renters, younger buyers and young families. The family component lifts the centre of gravity closer to 30-40 than to a pure under-30 BTR profile.
- Sources: [Royal Wharf community](https://www.royalwharf.com/neighbourhood/community), [Royal Wharf local postcode demographics](https://www.crystalroof.co.uk/report/postcode/E161GB/demographics)

### Kidbrooke Village

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Kidbrooke Village is likely the most family-leaning place in the set below the premium central schemes. The borough profile is still working-age heavy, but the park-led environment and suburban calm strongly point to 30-40 as the dominant band.
- Sources: [Royal Greenwich facts and figures](https://www.royalgreenwich.gov.uk/info/200245/facts_and_figures_about_greenwich), [Kidbrooke Village](https://www.kidbrookevillage.co.uk/about-kidbrooke-village/), [Kidbrooke local postcode demographics](https://www.crystalroof.co.uk/report/postcode/SE39FW/demographics)

### Oval Village

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Project / development`
- Why: Oval Village is a premium Berkeley project in Zone 1. Unlike the larger mixed-use districts, it does not derive a large student or cultural-footfall bias. Pricing and owner-occupier appeal push it toward established professionals, though not as far upmarket and older-skewing as the true trophy addresses.
- Sources: [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf), [Oval Village](https://www.ovalvillage.co.uk)

### Brent Cross Town

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Barnet as a borough is somewhat older than the east and inner-south locations in this guide, but Brent Cross Town itself is being built to attract younger renters, new households and future campus-linked activity. The likely resident mix is younger than the borough average, but still looks more 30-40 than 18-30 today.
- Sources: [Barnet borough profile and census](https://www.barnet.gov.uk/your-council/open-data-and-transparency/borough-profile-and-census), [Brent Cross Town](https://www.brentcrosstown.co.uk/place)

### Canada Water

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Area / masterplan`
- Why: Canada Water combines strong Zone 2 connectivity, large-scale redevelopment, leisure programming and office delivery. That points to a younger working-age base with broad visitor footfall, but the dominant long-term profile still looks more 30-40 than under-30.
- Sources: [Southwark JSNA demographics](https://www.southwark.gov.uk/health-and-wellbeing/public-health/insight-and-data/JSNA/demographics), [Canada Water](https://canadawater.co.uk)

### Embassy Gardens

- Product tag: `40+`
- Place type: `Project / development`
- Why: Embassy Gardens is premium, security-adjacent and globally marketed. The lifestyle is more mature affluent-professional than first-job renter, so the centre of gravity sits above the younger masterplan districts and lands most convincingly in 40+.
- Sources: [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf), [Embassy Gardens](https://www.ecoworldballymore.com/developments/london/embassy-gardens)

### Bloom Nine Elms

- Product tag: `30-40`
- Place type: `Project / development`
- Why: Bloom is a rental-led product with amenities and management aimed squarely at younger professional households. It is premium, but the tenure structure keeps it younger than nearby ownership-led addresses while still feeling more 30-40 than under-30 overall.
- Sources: [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf), [Bloom Nine Elms](https://www.bloomnineelms.com)

### Riverlight Quay

- Product tag: `40+`
- Place type: `Project / development`
- Why: Riverlight is a more ownership-oriented riverside address than the BTR-led schemes nearby. The combination of pricing, unit profile and lower youth-oriented programming suggests an older resident base than Bloom or Wembley Park, with 40+ the more convincing dominant tag.
- Sources: [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf), [Riverlight Quay](https://www.riverlight-quay.com)

### One Nine Elms

- Product tag: `40+`
- Place type: `Project / development`
- Why: Hotel-serviced luxury towers almost always skew older and wealthier than mainstream new-build stock. This looks much more like an established-buyer and international-owner product than a young-renter building, so 40+ is the cleaner dominant signal.
- Sources: [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf), [One Nine Elms by KPF](https://www.kpf.com/project/one-nine-elms)

### King's Cross - Gasholders & Capella

- Product tag: `40+`
- Place type: `Signature address`
- Why: This is not the general King's Cross population. It is the ownership tier inside one of London's most supply-constrained premium districts. The likely resident mix is older, wealthier and much less transient than the wider area.
- Sources: [King's Cross live, work, study](https://www.kingscross.co.uk/live-work-study), [Gasholders at King's Cross](https://www.kingscross.co.uk/gasholders)

### Battersea Power Station - Switch House

- Product tag: `40+`
- Place type: `Signature address`
- Why: Like the Gasholders, this is a scarcity ownership product rather than a broad district demographic. Pricing, uniqueness and ownership orientation all point strongly toward the 40+ band as the dominant resident cohort.
- Sources: [Battersea Power Station homes](https://batterseapowerstation.co.uk/live/homes/), [Wandsworth JSNA people chapter](https://www.wandsworth.gov.uk/media/14626/jsna-full-chapter-2-people.pdf)
