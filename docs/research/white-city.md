## Proposed Flagship Projects

- **White City Living** — 2,500+ homes, 8 acres of parks and gardens, and a very aggressive amenity package including pool, spa, gym, cinemas, work lounges, and a rooftop beach club.  
  Source: https://www.berkeleygroup.co.uk/developments/london/white-city/white-city/

- **The Solaris One & Two** — the premium White City Living phase with the rooftop pool, beach club, sunset bar, and the clearest premium skyline-facing proposition inside the Berkeley masterplan.  
  Source: https://www.berkeleygroup.co.uk/developments/london/white-city/white-city/the-solaris-one-and-two

- **Television Centre** — the heritage-led mixed-use core of the area, with homes, offices, restaurants, Electric Cinema, White City House, Soho House gym and pool, and BBC legacy built into the place identity.  
  Source: https://televisioncentre.com/ ; https://televisioncentre.com/television-centre/

- **Scenery House & The Ariel** — the current and final Television Centre residential launches, positioned next to Hammersmith Park and representing the strongest current ownership product within the TVC portion of White City.  
  Source: https://televisioncentre.com/ ; https://televisioncentre.com/homes/

## Tags Not Recommended

- **European-led** — not rigorous enough against current ward evidence. White City is visibly international, but the safest evidence-backed tag is `Mixed`.
- **30-40** — not the strongest age signal. The broader ward skews younger, with 20-29 as the largest band.
- **Accessible / Mid-range** — not persuasive relative to the rest of this shortlist. Current official pricing and live rental evidence place White City in the premium bracket.
- **Ready now** — too optimistic for a district that is already working but still plainly mid-delivery.

## Summary

White City deserves to be in the shortlist. It has the polish, management quality, and amenity density of the stronger masterplanned entries, but unlike some of the calmer or more settled premium districts it also has a younger and more socially active feel. The combination of Westfield, Imperial, Television Centre, White City House, and Berkeley's amenity-heavy residential product gives it genuine destination energy rather than the quieter feel of a pure residential enclave.

The cleanest way to model it in this project is as a district-scale `Area / masterplan` entry with `Zone 2`, `Rent or buy`, `Premium`, `In delivery`, `Parkside`, `Destination`, `Mixed`, `18-30`, and `Well Connected`.

## Confidence

High on zone, price, status, character, community, place type, and flagship project selection. Medium-high on demographic and age because those rely on ward-level evidence rather than a resident census specific to White City Living or Television Centre.
