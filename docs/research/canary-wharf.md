## Tags to remove or correct

- Change `East Asian & European` → `Mixed`
  - Why: Census 2021 shows Canary Wharf ward is 41.6% White and 41.9% Asian. The Asian population in Tower Hamlets is predominantly Bangladeshi (South Asian), not East Asian. The near-equal split between White and Asian residents, with significant Black (6.2%) and Mixed (5.0%) populations, makes "Mixed" the most accurate descriptor. The "East Asian & European" tag overstates the East Asian component and understates the South Asian presence.
  - Sources: https://www.citypopulation.de/en/uk/london/wards/tower_hamlets/E05009323__canary_wharf/

## Current state and liveability

Canary Wharf / Wood Wharf is a major Zone 2 waterside destination that has been transformed by the Elizabeth line, which puts Tottenham Court Road at 11 minutes and Farringdon at 9 minutes. The Vertus BTR operation is professionally managed with strong amenities, and the purchase market ranges from mid-range to ultra-premium via One Park Drive. Wood Wharf is still building out Phase Three, which keeps the "In delivery" tag accurate. The demographic tag needs correction: Census 2021 data for the ward shows a near-equal White/Asian split with the Asian component predominantly Bangladeshi (South Asian), not East Asian, making "Mixed" the most defensible label.

## Source-quality note

Evidence quality is high. TfL zone confirmation is authoritative. Census 2021 ward-level ethnicity data from citypopulation.de (sourced from ONS) provides clear percentages. Pricing is verified against Vertus's own site, Rightmove, OnTheMarket, and Chestertons. Travel times are sourced from traintickets.com and londondrum.com journey planners. The weakest point is the age mix: while ward-level Census data exists, the Canary Wharf ward includes both the financial district workers and Wood Wharf residents, so the age profile may not perfectly reflect the residential-only population.
