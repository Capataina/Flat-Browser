**Rental qualification notes for North Acton BTR operators:**
- **UNCLE** typically uses income-based referencing (annual income >= 30x monthly rent), accepts employed and self-employed tenants, and uses AST agreements. Credit checks are standard but UNCLE has historically been flexible with international tenants. Visa-friendliness: likely medium-to-good.
- **Enclave (Outpost)** operates as co-living with all-inclusive pricing. Co-living schemes in London generally have lighter referencing requirements than traditional ASTs — often accepting bank statements in lieu of payslips and being more accommodating of non-UK credit history. The all-inclusive model removes utility deposit friction. Visa-friendliness: likely good.
- **AWOL (One West Point BTR)** — insufficient data to assess qualification terms.

- **Agreement type:** AST (Assured Shorthold Tenancy), 3-24 month terms
- **Referencing provider:** Not specifically sourced; likely in-house or major provider (Goodlord, Canopy, etc.)
- **Guarantor:** Accepted for applicants who do not meet income threshold
- **All-inclusive:** Wi-Fi and utilities set up and ready; exact inclusion varies by building

**Rent increase concern:** Multiple Trustpilot reviews report significant annual rent increases. One resident reported a 30% YoY increase. This is a material risk for budget planning — Quintain Living's low entry pricing may not hold at renewal. The Renters' Rights Act 2025 will constrain in-tenancy increases to market rate via the new rent increase mechanism, but between-tenancy pricing (for new buildings on periodic tenancies) will still be market-driven.

**Key insight for graduate-visa renters:** The BTR sector is structurally more visa-friendly than the private landlord market because BTR operators use income-based referencing (which can accept offer letters or savings) rather than the credit-history-heavy approach of traditional referencing agencies. However, the income multiples (typically 30x monthly rent) still require demonstrable income — a job offer or substantial savings. The Renters' Rights Act 2025 advance rent cap will further narrow the options for visa-holders who currently use large upfront payments to bypass income requirements.
