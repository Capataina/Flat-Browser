## Current state and liveability

Kidbrooke Village is a well-built, parkside Zone 3 masterplan that consistently delivers good-quality homes at pricing that is accessible to mid-range for London. Cator Park integration, Berkeley build quality, and proximity to Blackheath village create a liveable suburban character. The main limitation is connectivity: Kidbrooke station offers only Southeastern rail services, with all onward tube/Elizabeth line connections requiring an interchange at London Bridge, which pushes average journey times to key London hubs above 30 minutes. The demographic profile is predominantly White (56.5%) but with significant Black (19.5%) and Asian (13.6%) minorities. Pricing spans from GBP 395,000 (1-bed) to GBP 1,100,000 (4-bed house), suggesting both "Accessible" and "Mid-range" tags are justified.

## Source-quality note

Evidence quality is medium. TfL zone data is clear. Census 2021 ethnicity data is sourced from citypopulation.de for the Charlton Hornfair ward (the 2021 successor to Kidbrooke with Hornfair), which is a reasonable proxy. Pricing is verified against live Rightmove and newhomesforsale.co.uk listings. The weakest areas are age mix (no 2021 Census age data located at ward level) and travel times (Kidbrooke station is not well-covered by online journey planners compared to tube stations, so some journey times are estimated from Southeastern timetables plus interchange assumptions).
