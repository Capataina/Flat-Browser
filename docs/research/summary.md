## Five Most Materially Inaccurate Entries

### 1. One Nine Elms — 3 tag corrections + 2 factual errors
- **Tenure** "Buy only" was wrong — extensive rental listings exist (up to £22,013/month)
- **Age** "40+" contradicted by Census (ward dominated by 20-39)
- **Developer** listed as Dalian Wanda but actual owner is CC Land Holdings since 2024
- **Hotel brand** described as Shangri-La but it is actually Park Hyatt London River Thames (opened October 2024)
- **Tower heights** stated as 46/56 storeys; actual is 42/57 per KPF and Wikipedia

### 2. Brent Cross Town — 2 tag corrections
- **Zone 4** was incorrect — both stations are Zone 3
- **Demographic** "European-led" contradicted by Census (Cricklewood ward: 43% White, no ethnic majority)

### 3. King's Cross Ownership — 3 tag corrections
- **Tenure** "Buy only" contradicted — Gasholders has active rental listings up to £8,000/month
- **Demographic** "European-led" contradicted (42% White ward, highly diverse)
- **Age** "40+" contradicted by Census (31% of ward is 20-29)

### 4. Battersea Switch House — 2 tag corrections
- **Tenure** "Buy only" contradicted — active Switch House lettings from studios to 4-beds
- **Age** "40+" contradicted by Census (Nine Elms ward dominated by 20-39)

### 5. Stratford / East Bank — 1 critical tag correction
- **Demographic** "South Asian-led" was the single most misleading tag in the dataset. Census 2021 shows White (43%), Asian (28%), and Black (18%) in a genuinely multi-ethnic ward within England's most diverse borough. "Mixed" is the only defensible tag.

## Summary Statistics

- **Total tag corrections applied**: 19 across 13 of 18 establishments
- **Establishments with no corrections needed**: Canada Water, Elephant Park, Bloom Nine Elms, Battersea Power Station, Wembley Park (only age changed)
- **Most common correction type**: Age (7 corrections) — the "40+" tag was systematically unsupported by Census data
- **Second most common**: Demographic (6 corrections) — "European-led" and "South Asian-led" tags were over-applied
- **Third most common**: Tenure (3 corrections) — "Buy only" was wrong for all three establishments tagged with it
- **New tag added**: Connectivity (all 18 establishments) — 9 Highly Connected, 7 Well Connected, 1 Moderately Connected, 1 Well Connected (borderline)

### Key Cross-Cutting Findings

1. **Every "Buy only" tag was incorrect.** One Nine Elms, KX Ownership, and BPS Switch House all have active rental listings. The secondary lettings market means no establishment in this dataset is truly purchase-only.

2. **The "40+" age tag was never substantiated by Census data.** All five establishments tagged "40+" shared wards dominated by 20-39 year olds. Premium pricing may attract older residents, but Census evidence rules required the correction.

3. **Demographic tags were the weakest category overall.** Six of eighteen needed correction. Ward-level Census data often doesn't perfectly match development boundaries, and rapidly growing wards (Nine Elms grew 14x, Wembley Park 7x) captured early-stage communities that may not reflect the mature resident base.

4. **The Elizabeth line has reshaped connectivity.** Royal Wharf jumped from what would have been "Moderately Connected" (DLR era) to "Highly Connected" (15 min average). This is the largest connectivity shift of any establishment in the dataset.

5. **Kidbrooke Village is the clear connectivity outlier.** It is the only "Moderately Connected" establishment, limited by Southeastern rail services and the requirement to interchange at London Bridge for all onward connections.
