**Note on age mix:** The "30-40" tag is defensible but sits on the boundary. Census data shows 20-29 as the largest ward-level group, and "18-30" could also be argued. The "30-40" tag is retained because Bloom's premium BTR pricing (studios from £2,675/month) positions it toward established professionals rather than entry-level renters, and the 30-39 Census group is close behind 20-29. This is a judgement call rather than a clear-cut verification.

## Summary

Bloom Nine Elms is the most cleanly verified establishment in this batch alongside Canada Water — every current tag is supported by available evidence. The only nuance is that Nine Elms ward's dominant age cohort is actually 20-29, not 30-39, making the age tag a reasonable-but-not-definitive fit. Connectivity is Well Connected at ~23-minute average.

The weakest tag is age mix (Census proxy is area-level, not Bloom-specific, and 20-29 slightly exceeds 30-39), but the tag is still defensible given the product's price positioning.
