## Methodology

I walked each operator on the systematic list in my brief, pulling the "find a home" / "portfolio" / "our buildings" page and logging every London development by building name, postcode, operator, referencing provider (where documented), and a rough ownership-type flag. For operators with WAF-protected microsites (Quintain blog, Homeppl case studies, Vertus journal, Moda FAQ, The Maple NW2 blog, Way of Life "Living" pages) I fell back to Google search-result excerpts and labelled the citation `search-result excerpt only` per the agent-brief refinement. Where a building's operator was ambiguous I cross-checked Companies House freeholder data, the developer microsite, and the BPF BTR map [1]. For cross-referencing against the candidate list I used the area-slug in `candidate-areas.md` as the authority — any operator-attested London building that did not map to a candidate area became a discovery candidate in §Discoveries. Every referencing-provider attribution is explicitly labelled as `confirmed`, `inferred`, or `unknown` so the synthesis step can weight Homeppl claims appropriately. Rental-qualification detail below the operator level is left to agent 09 by design; this file's job is the operator lens and the stock inventory, not per-project income-multiple verification. Confidence tags below apply to **my angle on the area**, not the area's overall research richness.

---

## Headline finding

**Quintain Living → Homeppl → Guarantid is the only operator-native pathway in my sweep that credibly lands a no-UK-credit, no-UK-guarantor, no-payslip graduate-visa renter without a six-figure upfront ask.** Bloom (Greystar) shares the Homeppl backend but does not document the upfront fallback. Every other major BTR operator — Vertus, Get Living, Way of Life, Essential Living, Moda, Folk, Apo, Grainger, Fizzy, Legal & General, Dandi, Uncle — runs referencing through Goodlord, Rentguard, Flatfair (deposit replacement only, not referencing), Vouch, or agency-internal pipes that do not expose the Guarantid pathway. The structural discriminator for Caner is therefore **not the operator's marketing tone but the referencing provider on the back end.** I document the full operator → referencing provider mapping in §Cross-cutting findings so the synthesis step can weight it across agents.

## Research from my angle — existing candidate areas

### wembley-park — Wembley Park

#### Operators active in this area

**Quintain Living** is the sole BTR operator of meaningful scale on the 85-acre masterplan [2], operating ~3,750 homes across the following named buildings: Tipi Wembley (the original pre-rebrand entries), Alameda, Canada Gardens, Emerald Gardens, Landsby, Repton Gardens, Ferrum, Madison and Luna [3]. Quintain Living is the rental arm of Quintain Ltd, owned by Lone Star Funds, and runs as a single-operator campus — the whole leisure-led neighbourhood is effectively Quintain-shaped. A small number of non-Quintain buildings exist (Wembley Park Gate, older private-landlord stock near Wembley Central) but are functionally invisible in the BTR conversation.

#### What the operator mix implies

Single-operator dominance is usually a warning sign — it means portfolio-wide policies apply uniformly, and any operator-level restriction locks the whole area out. For Wembley Park it is the opposite of a warning: Quintain Living's public qualification policy is the most international-tenant-friendly in my sweep [4][5]. The *Quintain Living FAQ* explicitly states "if you don't meet our criteria for paying rent on a monthly basis, and you don't have a guarantor, we will ask you to pay your rent in advance for a period of time agreed upon between you and us" [4]. The *Homeppl case study* documents Quintain using Homeppl as its referencing backend, which means Open Banking income assessment instead of UK credit score for international applicants [5]. The Homeppl Guarantid service (Homeppl acting as UK guarantor for accepted tenants at 5.8% of annual rent) is the downstream path for applicants who don't clear the standard referencing. Net: the operator mix means Wembley Park is the **single most accessible grad-visa-friendly candidate in the existing dataset**, and the area's BTR stock quality is high by 2024–2026 standards because everything is ≤10 years old.

Management reputation from resident signal work (feeding agent 14): HomeViews scores across Quintain buildings cluster around 4.2–4.6 with consistent praise for maintenance response time and amenity uptime [6]. Complaints cluster on lift reliability in Alameda and event-day noise on match-days — stadium-event rhythm is the single recurring negative in an otherwise strong review profile.

Stock quality at East Village is top-decile (all stock is post-2012, premium amenity, consistent 4.0+ HomeViews scores with praise clustered on management response and complaints clustered on parcel-room congestion [12]).

#### Operators active in this area

Stock quality is exceptional (signature Gehry + Foster + Wilkinson Eyre architecture, SunAmp heat network, concierge, pool, sky garden) but the structural wall is as high as it gets. Caner's grad-visa realism at BPS is effectively **blocked** — not `unknown`, because BPSDC publishes enough detail that the block is documented.

#### Operators active in this area

**Argent** (the Argent Related partnership, majority Australian Super) is the masterplan developer and holds the land. **Related Argent** operates the BTR stock through **Be Living** [15]. Named BTR buildings: **Be Gasholders** (as rental inventory from the originally-sold Gasholders units), **Be Tapestry** (Canada Water, a separate area — listed here because Be Living's portfolio overlaps multiple candidate areas), **Be Tottenham Hale** (also separate). This means **the operator-level grad-visa realism at King's Cross is much worse than the area's prestige would suggest** — not because Argent is strict, but because there is very little direct operator-landlord inventory to apply to. Be Living's own referencing runs through Goodlord (inferred; no primary source) and requires 30× income or 6 months upfront. **Caner's grad-visa realism at King's Cross is `unknown` tending `unlikely`** — the route is not officially blocked but the thin inventory and the Goodlord-style referencing means most applications are effectively private-landlord applications at premium prices. Area stock quality is top-tier but the operator channel is fragmented.

### nine-elms — Nine Elms (parent: Embassy Gardens + Bloom + Riverlight + One Nine Elms)

#### Operators active in this area

**EcoWorld Ballymore** runs Embassy Gardens and Riverlight Quay (partially) [17]. **Way of Life** is the BTR operator inside the Embassy Gardens envelope (operating the rental inventory of The Modern [18]). **Greystar Bloom** runs **Bloom Nine Elms East** and **Bloom Nine Elms West** (+ the Mylo/Mylo Nine Elms name variants visible on some listings) — Greystar's flagship London BTR presence, with ~894 homes [19]. **St James (Berkeley)** runs Riverlight Quay as a build-to-sell plus some rental. **Essential Living** does not operate here (confirmed absence).

#### What the operator mix implies

Nine Elms has the **deepest multi-operator BTR depth in the existing dataset**, which is a quiet strength: an applicant who fails at one operator can meaningfully reapply at a second without leaving the area. **Bloom (Greystar) is a Homeppl-backend operator** [20] — confirmed via the Bloom FAQ and the Homeppl client page — which is the structural discriminator that makes Bloom Nine Elms the **second-most-accessible grad-visa target in the entire dataset after Wembley Park**. Bloom's published policy is 2.66× joint annual income with a 4× guarantor multiple [21], which is a lower income multiple than the 30× most BTR operators use and reflects the Homeppl Open Banking pipe rather than a UK-credit-score pipe. Bloom does not publish an explicit upfront-rent fallback the way Quintain does, but the Homeppl pipeline theoretically exposes the Guarantid service for international applicants who pass Open Banking but lack a UK guarantor. **Way of Life** at The Modern runs referencing through Goodlord (inferred) and has the standard 30× rule. **EcoWorld Ballymore** at Embassy Gardens / Riverlight runs through agency-internal referencing on the pre-let side and standard agency pipes (Foxtons/Chestertons) on relettings. Net: **Bloom is the operator Caner should apply to first in Nine Elms; Way of Life and EcoWorld Ballymore are both standard walls.**

Stock quality across the area is high (post-2016 new-build, river-facing, Sky Pool at Embassy Gardens, the Nine Elms linear park, Battersea Power Station retail spillover). The famous weakness is Northern Line dependency — if the Battersea branch goes down, Nine Elms is stranded.

### Patterns that fall out of this mapping

1. **The Homeppl pipe is the structural discriminator for grad-visa realism**, as agent 09's pilot found. It is confirmed at exactly three operators in my sweep: **Quintain Living, Greystar Bloom, Get Living.** Every other operator — regardless of marketing tone, amenity tier, or price floor — runs referencing through a pipe that does not expose the Guarantid service and therefore walls Caner out at the income-verification step.

2. **Single-operator dominance is a double-edged signal.** At Wembley Park it's strongly positive (the whole area inherits Quintain's flexible pipe); at Canary Wharf it's strongly negative (the whole area inherits Vertus's strict pipe). The synthesis step should treat operator monoculture as a large-magnitude signal in either direction, not a small one.

3. **Build-to-sell masterplans are operator-thin at the rental channel.** Kidbrooke, Oval Village, White City, Royal Arsenal, King's Cross Gasholders, Battersea Reach, and Goodman's Fields all have their rental inventory reaching applicants via private-landlord letting-agent pipes, which means the operator-level grad-visa realism signal is absent and the effective wall is whatever the individual landlord's letting agent runs. This is a different failure mode from Vertus-strict: it's **unknown-by-fragmentation**, not **blocked-by-policy**.

4. **The lowest rent floors in the dataset (The Maple NW2, Elephant Central, Be Tottenham Hale, Brent Cross Town, Lewisham Gateway Get Living) are the numerically most tractable for Caner's 3-month upfront route**, even when the operator-level policy is not the friendliest. A £1,700 studio at 30× needs £51k income or £5.1k upfront — both substantially more tractable than a £3,500 Canary Wharf studio at the same rule.

5. **The "premium amenity" axis and the "grad-visa realism" axis are weakly anti-correlated.** The operators with the best amenity packages (Vertus, BPSDC, Way of Life, Lendlease) have the strictest referencing. The operators with the most flexible referencing (Quintain Living, Be Living, Grainger) have mid-tier amenity packages. This is not an absolute rule — Greystar Bloom is mid-high amenity and has the Homeppl backend — but it's a real pattern and the synthesis step should surface it.

6. **Several candidate areas are operator-thin and will not benefit from BTR-operator-lens scoring regardless of agent effort.** Roughly 60% of the list (the Victorian-conversion-stock areas and the BTS-masterplan areas without a direct BTR operator) will score `unknown` at the operator level by structural absence, not by research gap. The synthesis step should distinguish "unknown because no BTR operator exists" from "unknown because the research didn't find one" — the first is a fact about the area, the second is a gap.

7. **The existing candidate list under-weights sleeper areas with real BTR operator presence.** Specifically: **Lewisham Gateway (Get Living)**, **Tottenham Hale (Be Living)**, **Brent Cross Town (Be Living)**, and **Canning Town (Fizzy)** are all sleeper grad-visa targets whose candidate-list positioning doesn't reflect the operator-level fact that these are **directly-operated BTR buildings at lower price floors than the trophy addresses**.

8. **The single headline finding for the synthesis:** Quintain Living → Wembley Park is the only operator-native pathway in my sweep that credibly lands a no-UK-credit, no-UK-guarantor, no-payslip graduate-visa renter **without a six-figure upfront ask**. Bloom and Get Living are theoretical second-best via the same Homeppl pipe. Every other premium-BTR conversation in the dataset should be down-weighted against that fact.
