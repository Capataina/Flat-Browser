# Sweep agent 19 — Flexible & Short-Term Tenancy BTR Operators in London

Research date: 2026-04-12
Operators reviewed: 18
Focus: Which BTR operators offer tenancy terms under 6 months, rolling monthly contracts, or flexible-living products — and how those interact with Caner's graduate-visa, no-UK-credit, no-payslip profile.

---

## Methodology

I walked each major BTR operator's public-facing website (FAQ, "how renting works", terms and conditions pages) and supplemented with industry press coverage, Wharf Life interviews, Short Term Rentalz, Urban Living News, and Property Week reporting. For operators whose microsites are WAF-protected, I used Google search-result excerpts and labelled citations accordingly. Where an operator's FAQ was silent on flexible terms, I recorded the standard minimum and flagged it as "no documented flexible pathway".

The research was structured around four questions per operator:

1. **Does this operator offer any tenancy under 6 months?** If so, what is the minimum?
2. **What is the price premium** (if any) for flexible vs standard AST?
3. **What are the referencing requirements** for flexible tenants — and does upfront payment bypass them?
4. **How international-tenant-friendly** is the pathway for someone with no UK credit history, no payslips, and a graduate visa expiring Aug 2027?

### Statutory context — the Renters' Rights Act 2025 (commencing 1 May 2026)

This research is written 18 days before the Renters' Rights Act 2025 commences on **1 May 2026**. Two provisions are directly relevant:

1. **Abolition of fixed-term ASTs.** From 1 May 2026, all assured tenancies in England become periodic (rolling monthly). Fixed terms are abolished. Tenants can leave with 2 months' notice at any time. This means that *every BTR tenancy created after 1 May 2026 is inherently "flexible"* in the sense that the tenant cannot be locked into a long term — but operators may still set minimum initial terms via contractual clauses that are not yet tested under the new regime [RRA-1][RRA-2].

2. **Upfront rent cap.** Landlords can require no more than 1 month's rent in advance for new tenancies created on or after 1 May 2026. The "pay 3-6 months upfront to bypass affordability" pathway — Caner's primary route — is **prohibited** for post-commencement tenancies [RRA-2][RRA-3]. Tenants may *voluntarily* prepay after the tenancy begins, but operators cannot require it.

**Net effect for this research:** The distinction between "flexible operator" and "inflexible operator" is about to narrow dramatically. Post-1 May 2026, the question shifts from "will this operator let me sign a 3-month AST?" to "will this operator reference me without UK credit history, given they can no longer require upfront rent as a mitigant?"

### 1. Quintain Living (Wembley Park)

**Buildings:** Alameda, Canada Gardens, Emerald Gardens, Landsby, Repton Gardens, Ferrum, Madison, Luna (~3,750 homes) [1]

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Minimum tenancy** | **3 months** [2] |
| **Maximum tenancy** | 24 months |
| **Standard tenancy** | 12 months (most residents) |
| **Price premium for short term** | Not publicly documented; likely higher rents on shorter terms based on industry practice |
| **Referencing provider** | Homeppl (Open Banking + Guarantid) [3] |
| **Income requirement** | Household income >= 2.5x annual rent [2] |
| **Guarantor requirement** | UK-based, income >= 5x annual rent; only one per apartment [2] |
| **Upfront rent alternative** | "If you don't meet our criteria for paying rent on a monthly basis, and you don't have a guarantor, we will ask you to pay your rent in advance for a period of time agreed upon between you and us" [2] |
| **International-tenant pathway** | Virtual/group viewings for overseas applicants; Homeppl Open Banking assessment instead of UK credit score; Guarantid service (Homeppl acts as UK guarantor at ~5.8% of annual rent) [3][4] |
| **Deposit** | 5 weeks' rent (< GBP 50k/yr annual rent) or 6 weeks' (>= GBP 50k/yr), TDS-protected [2] |
| **Deposit-free option** | Yes — deposit-free renting scheme documented [5] |

**Assessment for Caner:**
Quintain is the standout operator for flexible tenancy. The 3-month minimum is the shortest documented standard offering among major London BTR operators. The Homeppl/Guarantid pathway is the only operator-native route that credibly lands a no-UK-credit, no-payslip graduate-visa applicant without requiring a huge upfront payment. The Guarantid fee (~5.8% of annual rent, roughly GBP 1,200-1,500 on a GBP 1,700/mo studio) is a meaningful cost but replaces the otherwise-blocked guarantor requirement entirely. **Post-RRA (after 1 May 2026), Quintain's Homeppl-backed Open Banking referencing becomes even more important** because the upfront-rent bypass is no longer available — Homeppl's ability to assess overseas bank statements via Open Banking is the remaining path to passing referencing without UK credit history.

**Confidence: high.** Primary-source FAQ quotes plus Homeppl case study.

**Assessment for Caner:**

The buildings are spread across East/South-East London, giving geographic coverage that Quintain (Wembley-only) lacks.

**Confidence: medium-high.** The 3-month minimum is confirmed by multiple industry press sources (Short Term Rentalz, Show House, Urban Living News) but Fizzy's own FAQ still says 12 months, which may indicate the policy is discretionary rather than universally available.

## Cross-cutting analysis

### Referencing provider is the structural discriminator

The single most important finding of this research — consistent with sweep-11 — is that **the referencing provider determines accessibility more than the tenancy length**. Three providers dominate the London BTR market:

| Provider | Approach | International-friendly? | Used by |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Homeppl** | Open Banking income assessment + Guarantid guarantor service | **Yes** — designed for applicants without UK credit history | Quintain Living, Fizzy Living, Bloom (Greystar) |
| **Goodlord** | Traditional credit check + employment verification | **No** — requires UK credit history | Way of Life, and likely several others |
| **Canopy** | Experian credit score threshold | **No** — explicit score floor (561+) | Moda Living |
| **Reference My Tenant (RMT)** | Traditional credit check | **No** — CCJ/adverse credit check | Apo |
| **Unknown/in-house** | Varies | Unknown | UNCLE, Get Living, Essential Living, Grainger, L&G, Dandara, Lendlease |

**Operators using Homeppl (Quintain, Fizzy, Bloom) are the only ones with a documented pathway for applicants without UK credit history.** Every other provider defaults to UK credit scoring, which Caner will fail.

### Post-RRA landscape (after 1 May 2026)

The Renters' Rights Act 2025 fundamentally reshapes this comparison:

1. **All tenancies become periodic.** Every new tenancy after 1 May 2026 is automatically rolling monthly. The tenant can leave with 2 months' notice at any time [RRA-1][RRA-2]. This means the *minimum term* distinction above becomes largely academic — no operator can lock a tenant into a fixed term.

2. **Upfront rent capped at 1 month.** The "pay 6-12 months upfront to bypass affordability" pathway is dead for post-commencement tenancies [RRA-2][RRA-3]. This eliminates Caner's primary alternative route at Way of Life, Apo, and any other operator that used bulk upfront payment as a mitigant.

3. **Referencing becomes the sole gatekeeper.** With fixed terms gone and upfront rent capped, the only remaining question is "can you pass referencing?" This makes the Homeppl operators (Quintain, Fizzy, Bloom) even more structurally important because they are the only ones whose referencing can work without UK credit history.

4. **BTR operators may resist very short stays.** The Act creates a scenario where a tenant could sign a tenancy and leave after 2 months (the minimum notice period). BTR operators face increased vacancy risk and may respond with stricter referencing to compensate for the lost ability to require long commitments [RRA-4]. This could paradoxically make it *harder* for marginal applicants to be accepted, even though the tenancy structure is technically more flexible.

5. **Voluntary prepayment loophole.** Tenants may *choose* to prepay rent after the tenancy begins [RRA-2]. Some operators may informally signal that voluntary prepayment would be welcomed, effectively preserving the old pathway through a legal fiction. Whether this becomes standard practice is unknown as of April 2026.
