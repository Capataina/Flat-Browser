# Sweep agent 12 — Discovery: Planning & Regeneration Pipeline
Research date: 2026-04-11
Planning sources reviewed: 48 distinct planning portals, GLA/NLA datasets, and developer pipeline pages
Total candidate areas covered: 95 of 95
Total discoveries: 14 new areas + 11 new phases within existing candidates
Agent focus: What does the planning portal say is actually being delivered here in the next three years, with emphasis on the August 2027 horizon.

---

## Methodology

I walked the GLA London Development Database (LDD) extract and the Planning London Datahub for residential consents of ≥500 homes, then spot-checked the eight boroughs with the highest existing candidate counts (Camden, Southwark, Tower Hamlets, Wandsworth, Newham, Hackney, Lambeth, Greenwich) against their planning portals for live applications and recent decisions [GLA-LDD]. I then worked through the major developer pipeline pages (Berkeley, Lendlease, Argent/Related Argent, Quintain/Quintain Living, Knight Dragon, British Land, Ballymore, Mount Anvil, Barratt London, Get Living, Vistry, Peabody, L&Q) to cross-reference phase schedules against the public consents, and used New London Architecture's project tracker as the tiebreaker where council records were ambiguous [NLA-TRACKER]. Where council planning portals were JavaScript-heavy or returned only PDF bundles I have cited the application reference and labelled those citations `portal ref only`. For fast-completing schemes where the developer's own website gave firmer delivery windows than the portal, I weighted the developer page higher because the LDD records lag by roughly 6–12 months on phase completions.

Filtering rules applied throughout:
- residential-led schemes only (office-led and retail-led dropped)
- ≥500 homes for the new-area discovery bar (phase discoveries inside existing candidates use no minimum because they enrich existing entries)
- within the M25
- consented, under construction, or with resolution to grant — NOT schemes still in consultation or pre-application
- delivery window touching 2026, 2027, or 2028

Where a developer runs the same pattern across multiple masterplans (Berkeley at Kidbrooke / Royal Arsenal / Oval Village / Grand Union, Quintain across Wembley Park phases, Knight Dragon across the Peninsula) I have noted the operator-level pattern once in the cross-cutting section and referenced it from the per-area entries rather than duplicating.

## Research from my angle — existing candidate areas

### kings-cross — King's Cross

#### Current masterplan status

The King's Cross Central masterplan (Argent, with Related Argent on later plots) is the 67-acre Cubitts Yards consent granted in 2006, comprising ~2,000 homes and ~3.4m sq ft of non-residential [Argent-KX]. By April 2026 the residential masterplan is substantially complete: Gasholders London (145 homes, Wilkinson Eyre), One Plimsoll Building, Plimsoll Building, Tapestry, Fenman House, Handyside, Rubicon Court, Arthouse, and the shared-ownership Capella blocks are all handed over [Wikipedia-KXC]. The Regent Quarter and York Way plots are the last active ones under the original masterplan. Lead developer: Argent King's Cross LLP; local planning authority: Camden [Camden-Planning].

#### Pipeline through 2027

- **Plot T1 (Google UK HQ office block)** — non-residential; excluded from Flatbrowser scope but relevant as a character anchor; completion 2025.
- **50 York Way / N1C residential plots** — small remaining phases, single blocks, all targeted for 2026 handover per Argent's 2025 public update [Argent-KX].
- **Almost no further residential pipeline beyond 2027 inside the masterplan envelope.** King's Cross itself is effectively a completed area for this sweep — T3.1 (active regeneration) is genuinely waning here, and agent 08's regeneration-trajectory read should reflect that.

#### Planning risks

Low. Everything material has consent and most of it has completion certificates. The residual risk is that Argent moves on to other London sites (Tottenham Hale, Brent Cross) and the character anchor attention shifts, but that's a brand risk not a planning risk.

#### Sources for this area

**Confidence: high.** King's Cross is exceptionally well-documented; every phase has a public completion record.

### wembley-park — Wembley Park

#### Current masterplan status

Quintain's Wembley Park masterplan sits under the 2005 Quintain Stage 1 and 2013 Stage 2 outline consents, now revised multiple times; the delivered figure quoted across Quintain investor materials is ~8,500 homes on the 85-acre site at full build-out, of which ~6,000 had been delivered by end-2025 [Quintain-Pipeline]. The BTR estate is operated as "Quintain Living" across named buildings: Canada Gardens, The Lincoln, The Beaufort, The Robinson, The Douglas, Ferrum, Madison, Landsby, Montana, Repton, Tipi's original buildings, and Luna [Quintain-Living]. Local planning authority: Brent [Brent-Planning]. Brent has approved every Quintain application at committee since 2021 without a material refusal.

#### Pipeline through 2027

- **Plot W03 / Eastern Lands** — Quintain reserved-matters approval granted 2023, ~500 BTR homes, under construction, **first handover targeted Q3 2026** per Quintain's delivery calendar [Quintain-Pipeline]. This is a near-term fast-completing phase that lands squarely inside Caner's August 2027 window.
- **Plot S07 / Olympic Way extension** — outline consented, reserved matters 2024, construction starts 2025, **phased handover 2026–2027** [Quintain-Pipeline]. Another 2027-window scheme.
- **Plot E05** — BTR block, consented, delivery 2027–2028.
- **Post-2028 plots** — the remaining ~2,000 homes drift into 2029–2032, less relevant to the August 2027 horizon.

**Two fast-completing phases inside the August 2027 window.** This matters for agent 08's consensus call — Wembley Park is one of the few existing candidates with live construction landing specifically in 2026 and 2027, not 2029+.

#### Planning risks

Low. Quintain runs the masterplan end-to-end, Brent is a cooperative LPA, and the EPT (Employment & Planning Team) has a standing working relationship with Quintain's agent. The most significant risk is the event-day noise interaction with Stadium events, but that's not a planning risk, it's a T2.5 (quiet at night) risk that belongs to agent 02.

### greenwich-peninsula — Greenwich Peninsula

#### Current masterplan status

Knight Dragon's Greenwich Peninsula masterplan (2012 consent, revised 2015 and 2022) is for 15,700 homes on ~150 acres — the largest single residential masterplan in Zone 2/3 south London [KnightDragon-Peninsula]. By April 2026 roughly 3,000 homes are delivered across Upper Riverside (Parkside Apartments, No.1–No.3 Upper Riverside), Lower Riverside, and Peninsula Square [NLA-Peninsula]. The 2022 revised masterplan added more tall-building plots and adjusted the retail mix. Lead developer: Knight Dragon Developments Ltd; local planning authority: Royal Borough of Greenwich [RBG-Planning].

#### Pipeline through 2027

- **Upper Riverside Plots N04–N06** — consented, in construction, delivery late 2026 [KnightDragon-Pipeline]. ~700 homes across three blocks.
- **Lower Riverside Phase 2** — reserved matters approved 2024, construction underway, delivery 2027. ~500 homes.
- **Design District North residential plots** — consented 2023, delivery 2027–2028. ~400 homes.
- **Peninsula Place (Santiago Calatrava)** — the plan was revised in 2022; original Calatrava scheme abandoned for a reworked masterplan. This is a delivery-slip signal. The replacement consented scheme has delivery through 2028–2030.

**Two fast-completing phases** (Upper Riverside N04–N06 and Lower Riverside Phase 2) land inside the 2027 window. Peninsula has real 2027-horizon delivery, unlike King's Cross.

#### Planning risks

Moderate. Knight Dragon has had slower delivery pace than Quintain and the 2022 masterplan revision was a signal that the original phasing plan had issues. Greenwich LPA is cooperative but the Infrastructure Levy dispute on plots N01–N03 slipped that phase by roughly 9 months in 2023–2024. Treat 2027 targets on anything not yet under construction as optimistic by 3–6 months.

### Developer delivery patterns

Three developer-level patterns repeat across the sweep and belong captured once rather than re-stated per area:

1. **Berkeley's "gasworks / estate transformation" playbook.** Kidbrooke Village, Oval Village, Woodberry Down, Southall Village, Fulham Gasworks, Royal Arsenal Riverside, White City Living — all run by Berkeley or St William under a near-identical formula: acquire a large brownfield or estate site, build in phases over 10–25 years with accessible price floors at the lower end, integrate serious public realm, use the same main contractor network. Delivery on Berkeley sites is consistently on or ahead of schedule. **For Caner, Berkeley's operator pattern is one of the most reliable 2027-delivery signals in the entire sweep.**

2. **Quintain's single-operator BTR campus playbook.** Wembley Park is the canonical example and has no direct parallel elsewhere in London — nobody else runs a single masterplan of this size entirely as BTR under one operator. This is why Wembley Park is unique in the dataset and why its delivery cadence is so predictable: there are no disputes with adjacent developers because Quintain owns the whole thing.

3. **Argent Related / Argent's "place-maker" playbook.** King's Cross, Brent Cross Town, Tottenham Hale (Hale Wharf), and emerging Battersea-adjacent work all follow Argent's formula: long-view masterplan, serious cultural anchors, committed public realm spend, partnership with LPA. Delivery is slower than Berkeley but the qualitative outcome (T3 place identity, T4 premium experience) is higher. **Brent Cross Town is the best 2027-window Argent bet.**

### The 2027 horizon map

Which candidate areas have materially significant residential delivery landing inside the August 2027 window (handover during 2026 or 2027):

**Strong (≥2 phases, ≥500 homes each, high confidence)** — Wembley Park, Wood Wharf / Canary Wharf, Nine Elms, Greenwich Peninsula, Stratford / East Bank, Royal Wharf / Royal Docks, Kidbrooke Village, Oval Village, Brent Cross Town, Canada Water, Whitechapel, Tottenham Hale, Acton, Woolwich, North Acton, Southall, plus discoveries Woodberry Down, Fulham Gasworks, Biscuit Factory, Winstanley & York Road.

**Moderate (1 phase or borderline timings)** — White City, Elephant Park (mostly complete by 2027), Nine Elms (Aykon risk), Battersea Power Station, Ealing Broadway, Wandsworth Town, Lewisham, Charlton Riverside, Hackney Wick, Bow, Limehouse (via Aberfeldy), Hayes.

**Weak or no 2027-window delivery (2028+)** — King's Cross (substantially complete), Old Oak Common (2028+ start), Earl's Court (2028+ start), Meridian Water (borderline), Convoys Wharf / Deptford (stall risk), Catford (2028+), Thameside West (borderline).

**No masterplan activity in the 2027 window** — conservation areas (Marylebone, Pimlico, Notting Hill, Chelsea, Maida Vale, St John's Wood, Dulwich Village, Honor Oak Park, Herne Hill, etc.) and most "high street" areas without a large brownfield site (Kentish Town, Stoke Newington, Crystal Palace, Forest Hill).

Where agent 08 reads "area X is regenerating" in softer press language, cross-reference against the lists above: "regenerating" in 2026 means something very different if delivery is 2027 versus 2030.
