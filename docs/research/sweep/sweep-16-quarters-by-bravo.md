## Methodology

Each building was researched separately using the operator's official website (thequarters.co.uk), individual suite pages, the Bravo Investment House corporate site, Buildington planning records, HomeViews verified resident reviews, Trustpilot reviews, Rightmove/Zoopla/OnTheMarket rental listings, and third-party sources (BTP Group project notes, Vascroft contractor portfolio, DevelopCroydon). Where the official site withheld pricing, current asking rents were taken from portal listings and cross-referenced against review mentions. Move-in requirements were extracted from the operator's published Terms & Conditions page and cross-checked against Trustpilot reviewer accounts. Confidence tags apply per-building. Every claim is sourced; items that could not be independently verified are flagged `[UNVERIFIED]`.

## Cross-cutting policies (from Terms & Conditions)

The following policies are stated at operator level and apply across all four buildings unless a per-building exception is noted below.

### Move-in process
1. **Reservation fee**: one week's rate, payable by debit/credit card at offer stage. Non-refundable [4].
2. **Reference documents**: applicant must submit reference documents and right-to-rent confirmations within 48 hours of reservation confirmation [4].
3. **Right-to-rent check**: face-to-face meeting with the general manager to inspect original ID documents (passport or driving licence), per UK Home Office requirements [4].
4. **General manager review**: final sign-off after references and right-to-rent validation pass [4].
5. **Booking monies**: due 7 days before start date (rent pro-rata + utilities pro-rata + deposit). Only debit/credit card accepted — no cash, cheques, or giro [4].
6. **Check-in**: between 12:00 and 17:00 on start date [4].

### Deposit
- **Five weeks' rent equivalent**, protected with the Deposit Protection Service (DPS) [4].

### Credit checks
- The T&Cs do **not** explicitly mention credit checks or affordability assessments.
- However, a 2022 Trustpilot review ("Seb", May 2022) reports being charged a **£400 credit check fee** with no refund despite providing excellent references [5]. This suggests a credit check **is** conducted, at least at some locations, but the fee structure and whether it is still current is uncertain.
- **Assessment**: the claim that The Quarters does "no credit checks" is **not substantiated**. The T&Cs require "reference documents" without specifying what that entails, and at least one resident reports a credit check fee. Treat referencing as **present but opaque** — the operator does not publicly disclose the referencing provider or criteria. `[PARTIALLY VERIFIED — one Trustpilot data point from 2022; current practice UNVERIFIED]`

### Background checks
- No mention of DBS/background checks in the T&Cs or in any review. Right-to-rent document check is the only identity verification described [4].
- `[UNVERIFIED whether any criminal background check is performed]`

### Income proof / affordability
- Not mentioned in the T&Cs. The "reference documents" requirement is unspecified [4].
- One Trustpilot review (Michelle Carr, Jan 2024) reports being asked for **6 months' rent in advance plus 6 weeks' deposit** despite having references and a previous landlord reference, suggesting the operator may demand large upfront payments in lieu of passing traditional affordability criteria [5].
- **Assessment for graduate-visa renters**: the lack of transparent affordability criteria is a **double-edged sword** — there is no published income-multiple that would automatically exclude Caner, but the operator may instead demand large upfront rent, which is effectively the same barrier in a different shape. The move-in cost could be as high as ~£6,000-£12,000+ depending on how many months' rent they require upfront.

### Minimum stay — operator-level statement
- The website homepage states: "stays for as short as 30 days" and "flexible, affordable and seamless rentals for periods of 3 to 6 months" [3].
- The DevelopCroydon listing describes stays "as brief as three months" [6].
- HomeViews reviews at Croydon mention: "You have to renew your contract every 6 months, even after living there for years" (Edmund Owusu, Nov 2025) [7].
- **Interpretation**: the standard contract is likely a **6-month AST** with 1-month notice at the end. Shorter stays (30-day to 3-month) are available but may be on a serviced-living licence rather than an AST, likely at a higher nightly/weekly rate. Per-building details below.

### What's included in rent
- **Confirmed included**: furnished suite, Wi-Fi/fibre broadband, water, heating, 24/7 security, CCTV, lift access, housekeeping, maintenance, package collection [3][4].
- **Council tax**: appears to be **tenant's responsibility** in most cases. Listings on Rightmove/OnTheMarket for Croydon state "tenants liable for Council Tax and a £40 utility cost each month" [8]. However, at Watford (which operates more like a hotel), one listing mentions council tax included [9].
- **Electricity**: some listings suggest a fixed monthly utility charge (~£40/month at Croydon) rather than metered billing [8]. Other listings at Kilburn advertise "all bills included" [10].
- **Cleaning**: bi-weekly cleaning mentioned for Swiss Cottage [11]; housekeeping listed as an amenity at all locations.
- **Assessment**: bills inclusion is **not uniform across the portfolio**. It varies by building, suite type, and possibly contract type (AST vs serviced licence). Always confirm per-building.
