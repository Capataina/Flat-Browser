# Sweep agent 08 — Regeneration & 2027 Trajectory
Research date: 2026-04-11
Total areas covered: 95
Agent focus: T3.1 (active regeneration / visible investment) and T5.3 (long-term ascending trajectory across the August 2027 visa-transition horizon)

## Methodology

This file is a forward-looking pass at every candidate area in `context/references/candidate-areas.md` (~95 entries). The research question for each area is not "does it have regeneration" but specifically "where will the area be in August 2027, the month Caner's graduate visa expires and he either has sponsorship locked in or needs to re-plan the whole move". That framing makes the classification honest: an area with a spectacular masterplan that happens to complete in 2025 is a *worse* long-term bet than an area still delivering phases through 2028–2030, even if the 2025 version looks more finished at inspection time.

Sources consulted, in rough order of authority:

1. **Greater London Authority London Development Database** (https://data.london.gov.uk/dataset/planning-london-datahub) — the single most authoritative register of major permissions, phases under construction, and unit counts across London. GLA LDD entries are treated as primary sources on their own per sweep refinement rule 3.
2. **New London Architecture project tracker** (https://nla.london/projects) — NLA's running register of major London schemes, with delivery-date metadata maintained by their research team.
3. **Borough council planning portals** — Barnet, Brent, Camden, Ealing, Greenwich, Hackney, Hammersmith & Fulham, Haringey, Islington, Lambeth, Lewisham, Newham, Southwark, Tower Hamlets, Wandsworth, Westminster. Each portal's application search is the canonical record of what is consented, under construction, or stalled inside the borough.
4. **Developer portfolio / investor pages** for the five masterplan-level operators who show up across many areas: Berkeley Group, Argent (King's Cross), Lendlease (Elephant Park, Stratford, Euston), Quintain (Wembley Park), Ballymore (Wood Wharf, Royal Wharf, Embassy Gardens, London City Island), Knight Dragon (Greenwich Peninsula), Related Argent (Brent Cross Town), British Land (Canada Water, Paddington Central), Delancey / LCR (King's Cross ownership), Battersea Power Station Development Company (BPSDC), St William (Oval Village, Ebury Bridge, various gas-works sites), and EcoWorld London.
5. **Press archives** (Architects' Journal, Building Design, Estates Gazette, Property Week, FT, Times property desk, Evening Standard homes section, CoStar UK) for dated announcements of planning permissions, contract awards, and topping-out dates.
6. **NLDD-adjacent open datasets** (London Plan opportunity area schedules, GLA affordable housing programme grant decisions) for slippage context.

Approach decisions:

- **Every area gets the current status / pipeline / 2027-trajectory triple** even when the area has no masterplan at all. For mature areas with no active regeneration (Marylebone, Dulwich Village, St John's Wood) the trajectory paragraph becomes "stable, not ascending, not declining" — which is load-bearing information for Caner because a stable A area is a defensible long-term bet and an ascending-but-peaking B area is not.
- **Operator-level pattern collapsing is allowed** per sweep refinement rule 1. Where Berkeley, Argent, Lendlease, Quintain, Ballymore, Knight Dragon, Related, or St William runs the same delivery rhythm across multiple masterplans, I note the operator-level pattern once in the cross-cutting findings and reference it from each affected area rather than repeating.
- **2027 classification is categorical**: ascending / peaking-2025-2026 / plateaued / declining / N/A-not-regenerating. Each area gets exactly one tag. The tag is separate from the grade — a plateaued A is better than an ascending C on T3.1 alone, but worse on T5.3, which is the whole point of splitting the two rows.
- **I do not guess prices, demographics, or zones** — those are other agents' lanes. Where I reference those facts I link the relevant primary source anyway for cross-checking, but I do not treat them as my load-bearing claims.
- **Schedule slippage is assumed, not hidden**. London Plan opportunity areas routinely run 3–5 years behind their adopted timetables (see cross-cutting section). Where an area's official delivery plan says "phase X complete by 2026" I mark it "claimed 2026, realistic 2027–2028" and explain the slippage in the area's trajectory paragraph. Synthesis can weigh this against other agents' more optimistic reads.

Confidence is tagged per area within each entry as high / medium / low. High means I am drawing on multiple dated primary sources for the specific claim. Medium means the masterplan is well-known but specific phase dates are inferred from aggregate sources (GLA LDD + press summary) rather than fresh developer bulletins. Low means I have general-knowledge framing but no specific verifiable phase schedule in hand — these entries are the ones the synthesis step should weight lightest against other agents' dense coverage.

### wembley-park — Wembley Park

#### Current status
Quintain's Wembley Park masterplan is the single largest single-operator build-to-rent campus in the UK and is one of the most mature of the London opportunity-area masterplans. The 85-acre site around Wembley Stadium has been under active delivery since ~2005 and now contains a running BTR stock of several thousand homes across named buildings (Tipi-era originals such as Alto and Emerald Gardens, plus newer Quintain Living stock at Canada Gardens, The Robinson, Ferrum, Madison, Landsby, The Beton, Luna, Repton Gardens, and others). Public realm — Olympic Way, Market Square, Boxpark Wembley, London Designer Outlet, the Arena Square event frontage — is fully delivered and operational. The Sunday Times named Wembley Park "Best Place to Live in London" in 2025, which is the clearest external market validation of the place having arrived rather than still being under construction [1].

#### Pipeline
Quintain's published pipeline (https://www.quintain.co.uk/our-buildings/) continues to list phases under active delivery through the back half of the 2020s, but the centre of gravity has shifted from masterplan-build to stock maintenance, stabilisation, and lease-up of existing towers. The final set of Quintain Living buildings (the ones north of Fulton Road and towards the Barnet boundary) is under construction with phased handovers running into 2026–2027 per the developer's published schedule. Brent Council's planning register (https://pa.brent.gov.uk/online-applications/) carries the outstanding applications for the last consented phases. The Elizabeth House consolidation, car-park deck redevelopment, and the small remaining eastern plots are the main outstanding items.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Wembley Park is in the **peaking-2025-2026** band with a soft tail into 2027. The place exists, the amenity mix (Boxpark, Designer Outlet, Troubadour Theatre, Market Square food, BoxPark's replacement offers, gyms, supermarkets) is in place and working, and the Quintain Living BTR stack is at operational scale rather than pre-let. By August 2027 the last Quintain Living tower will either be open or within months of opening, and the masterplan will be effectively complete as a place even if the last crane has not quite come down. The 2027 version of Wembley Park is essentially the 2025 version plus a couple of extra towers and a matured Boxpark replacement — it is a much better bet than a peaking-and-then-declining area, but it is not still *ascending* in the way Brent Cross Town or Earls Court are. The net effect for Caner is that Wembley Park's T3.1 call is strong *today* but its T5.3 call is weaker than it would have been in 2022, and an area scoring equivalently on T1-T4 with a longer ascending runway is a better 2027 bet.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1 (active regeneration)**: **pass** — cranes still up on the final Quintain phases, masterplan delivery not quite complete, public realm ongoing (minor). Confidence: high.
- **T5.3 (ascending trajectory)**: **partial** — ascending but approaching its ceiling. Still broadly improving through 2026, plateauing in 2027. Confidence: high.
- Category: **peaking 2025-2026**, with a soft tail.

### greenwich-peninsula — Greenwich Peninsula

#### Current status
Knight Dragon's Greenwich Peninsula masterplan is the textbook example of a very large scheme (150 acres, ~17,000 homes headline figure) whose delivery has fallen *behind* its original timetable but remains unambiguously active. Upper Riverside is partially delivered; Lower Riverside is under construction; Central Park, Peninsula Square, and the Tide linear park are in place and working; the Design District and The O2 amenity cluster are operational. Knight Dragon publishes a running list of delivered and in-progress buildings at https://www.greenwichpeninsula.co.uk/live/.

#### Pipeline
The full delivery horizon for Greenwich Peninsula is ~2030s, with roughly half the masterplan still to build as of 2026. Knight Dragon has publicly re-phased the scheme more than once and the original 2020s-heavy timetable has slipped into 2030s territory. Greenwich Council's planning register (https://planning.royalgreenwich.gov.uk/online-applications/) confirms running applications for the next phases. Specific deliveries expected to complete or reach topping-out between now and August 2027 include Lower Riverside phases, additional buildings along Central Park, and the continued rollout of Design District retail tenants.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Greenwich Peninsula is **ascending through 2027** — one of the clearest cases in the dataset. The masterplan has about half its units still to deliver, the developer is still actively marketing new phases, and the Tide / Design District / O2 amenity cluster continues to mature as more residents move in and more retail tenants fit out. By August 2027 the place will have meaningfully more housing, meaningfully more retail activation, and meaningfully more public realm than in April 2026. The *rate* of change will be slowing as the masterplan grinds through the long tail, but it will still be improving year-on-year. This is a strong T5.3 pass.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **pass** — ongoing masterplan delivery, cranes clearly visible on site, consented pipeline not exhausted. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **strong pass** — ascending across August 2027 horizon with a clear pipeline. Confidence: high.
- Category: **ascending through 2027**.

### stratford-east-bank — Stratford / East Bank

#### Current status
Stratford is one of London's most successful regeneration stories and one of the few where the gap between the plan and the delivered reality has shrunk rather than grown. The Olympic Park (Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, https://www.queenelizabetholympicpark.co.uk/) is fully open, Westfield Stratford City is mature and trading, East Village (Get Living) is operational at full lease-up, International Quarter London (Lendlease) has delivered its commercial phases plus residential, and the East Bank cultural quarter is the main remaining in-flight piece. V&A East Storehouse opened in May 2025, V&A East Museum is scheduled to open spring 2026, UCL East (Marshgate, Pool Street) is open and operating, Sadler's Wells East opened February 2024, and BBC Music Studios is under fit-out. These are dated, verifiable deliveries rather than speculative timelines.

#### Pipeline
Stratford's headline pipeline items for the 2026-2027 window are the final V&A East Museum opening (scheduled 2026), BBC Music Studios opening (scheduled 2025-26), additional Chobham Manor / East Wick / Sweetwater housing phases by Taylor Wimpey and Places for London / London Legacy Development Corporation (LLDC, https://www.queenelizabetholympicpark.co.uk/planning-authority), and continued delivery of International Quarter London residential. LLDC is also pushing the long-tail delivery of Pudding Mill Lane, which is still very early and has most of its phases outside the 2027 horizon. Newham Council's planning portal (https://pa.newham.gov.uk/online-applications/) carries the area's non-LLDC applications.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Stratford is **ascending through 2027**, firmly. The East Bank story alone guarantees that August 2027 Stratford is meaningfully different from April 2026 Stratford — V&A East Museum opens inside the window, BBC Music Studios activates within the window, and the cultural quarter transitions from "impressive construction site" to "fully programmed destination". Underneath that, East Wick, Sweetwater, and Pudding Mill Lane residential phases keep delivering. This is one of the cleanest T5.3 passes in the whole dataset.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **strong pass** — East Bank delivery visibly active, LLDC still running, cranes on multiple sites. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **strong pass** — the 2027 version is materially better than the 2026 version, with dated events driving the change. Confidence: high.
- Category: **ascending through 2027**, with dated cultural activations inside the window.

### battersea-power-station — Battersea Power Station

#### Current status
BPSDC's Battersea Power Station redevelopment is far along. The Power Station building itself opened to the public in October 2022 with retail, Apple's UK HQ, hotel, and apartments; Phases 1 (Circus West Village, by Wilkinson Eyre/SimpsonHaugh, completed 2017), Phase 2 (the Power Station itself), Phase 3a (Prospect Place and Prospect Park, completed 2022-2023 with Frank Gehry / Foster + Partners design), and Battersea Roof Gardens are delivered. Battersea Power Station Underground station (Northern Line extension) has been operating since September 2021 (https://tfl.gov.uk/modes/tube/northern-line-extension). The masterplan is therefore closer to finished than not.

#### Pipeline
Remaining consented pieces in the masterplan include Electric Boulevard phases finishing out retail, residential phases 4 and onwards (Phase 4 at the western edge of the site, Phase 5 around the former Marco Polo House footprint), and the long-tail commercial components. Wandsworth Council planning portal (https://planning.wandsworth.gov.uk/online-applications/) has the running applications. The pace of new announcements has slowed meaningfully compared to the 2019-2023 build-out peak, and BPSDC has publicly re-cast financial projections on the later phases, which is consistent with a masterplan approaching its downhill slope.

#### Trajectory through 2027
BPS is in the **peaking-2025-2026** band, possibly sliding into **plateaued** by 2027. The Power Station itself, Apple HQ, the public realm, the Frank Gehry and Foster buildings, the tube station, and the primary retail are all in place and mature. The remaining residential phases will add marginal density and marginal tenancy but not a step change. By August 2027 BPS will look essentially the way it looks today, with one or two additional residential blocks and slightly more retail fit-out — not meaningfully ascending. It is also worth flagging that BPSDC's financial position has been the subject of repeated press coverage (FT, EG, Bloomberg) questioning the next-phase viability, which creates downside tail risk that a purely ascending-through-2027 call would understate.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **pass** — last residential phases still live, some cranes up. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **partial / weak** — the 2027 version is not meaningfully better than the 2026 version. Confidence: high.
- Category: **peaking 2025-2026**, edging toward plateaued.

### kings-cross — King's Cross

#### Current status
Argent's King's Cross Central masterplan (https://www.kingscross.co.uk/) is the gold-standard example of a completed opportunity area in London. The 67-acre site — Granary Square, Coal Drops Yard, Gasholders, Plimsoll Building, Bagley Walk, Lewis Cubitt Square, Pancras Square, King's Boulevard, and the Central Saint Martins campus — is delivered and mature. Argent handed the final residential plots to occupation across 2022-2024. The most recent major deliveries are the Google HQ (Heatherwick + BIG, https://www.bdcmagazine.com/tag/google-kings-cross/) which topped out in 2024 and is in fit-out, and the final phases of residential at the northern edge of the site.

#### Pipeline
The King's Cross masterplan has effectively no significant remaining phases to deliver. Google's fit-out will complete in 2025-2026, there are minor retail fit-outs in Coal Drops Yard, and Camden Council's planning register (https://accountforms.camden.gov.uk/planning-applications-and-notices) shows only small-scale applications inside the site. Argent itself has publicly turned attention to other projects (Brent Cross Town via its Related Argent joint venture) and the King's Cross site is in stewardship mode rather than build-out mode.

#### Trajectory through 2027
King's Cross is **plateaued** — it is the reference plateau against which other areas should be judged. The place is complete. Its character is set, its retail mix is mature, its public realm is the best in London, and its amenity is saturated. This does not make it a bad bet for Caner — a plateaued gold-standard area is strictly better than an ascending weak area on every daily-life dimension — but it does mean the T5.3 row cannot honestly be scored as ascending. The 2027 King's Cross will look essentially identical to the 2026 King's Cross. Caner's gain from living there is the *level*, not the *slope*.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **partial** — Google fit-out is the only visible thing still going, otherwise masterplan is complete. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **weak** — the area is not ascending, but it is also not declining. It is at the top of its arc. Confidence: high.
- Category: **plateaued (reference plateau)**.

### nine-elms — Nine Elms (Vauxhall / Nine Elms / Battersea opportunity area, Nine Elms segment)

#### Current status
Nine Elms is a large multi-developer opportunity area with delivery split across many operators: Embassy Gardens (EcoWorld Ballymore), Riverlight Quay (St James Berkeley), One Nine Elms (R&F), The Corniche (St James), Bloom Nine Elms, plus smaller ownership and mixed-tenure schemes. The Northern Line extension with stations at Nine Elms and Battersea Power Station has been operating since September 2021, which was the spine that the whole opportunity area was conditional on [1]. Most of the larger headline towers are either occupied or in the final fit-out stages.

#### Pipeline
Nine Elms has a long tail of smaller phases — the remaining Embassy Gardens plots at the western edge, later phases of the Vauxhall Square redevelopment, the Sky Gardens area, and the continuing build-out of the Sainsbury's-owned land at the northern edge. Wandsworth and Lambeth planning portals (https://planning.wandsworth.gov.uk/online-applications/ and https://planning.lambeth.gov.uk/online-applications/) have the running applications. The peak activity for Nine Elms was 2018-2023; the current period is long-tail.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Nine Elms is in the **peaking-2025-2026 → plateaued** band. The major deliveries have happened. The retail around Embassy Gardens (Sky Pool, local retail) is in place. The US Embassy is in place. The Linear Park is delivered. What remains is filling in remaining plots, which will add density and minor amenity but not a step change. By August 2027 Nine Elms will look essentially like today, with more residential stock and slightly more retail. It is a stable "arrived" area, not an ascending one.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **pass** — still some cranes, long tail of phases. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **partial** — improving, but slowly; not a meaningful 2027 upgrade over 2026. Confidence: high.
- Category: **peaking 2025-2026**.

### canary-wharf-wood-wharf — Canary Wharf / Wood Wharf

#### Current status
Canary Wharf Group's Canary Wharf estate is mature; Wood Wharf is the 23-acre eastward extension and is the active regeneration component. Wood Wharf's masterplan (https://www.canarywharf.com/our-vision/wood-wharf/) has delivered 10 Park Drive (the first residential tower, completed 2020), One Park Drive (Herzog & de Meuron, completed 2021, Vertus BTR), Newfoundland (SOM, Vertus BTR, completed 2021), 8 Water Street (Vertus BTR, completed 2022), plus Cuba Street and George Street public realm. Wood Wharf already has a working food scene (Mercato Metropolitano at Wood Wharf, Dishoom Wood Wharf has been announced, Notto Pasta Bar, etc.), a Third Space gym, and is transitioning from "construction site" to "neighbourhood".

#### Pipeline
Canary Wharf Group's public pipeline includes further Wood Wharf residential phases (towers west and east of the delivered cluster), additional office repurposing within the original estate (the ongoing life-sciences conversion at 20 Water Street and elsewhere, in response to post-pandemic financial-services floorspace demand), additional retail on Crossrail Place and the Wood Wharf promenade, and — importantly — the very-long-horizon conversions of former office blocks into residential (most visibly the proposed conversions of 10 and 20 Cabot Square / the Riverside South towers). Tower Hamlets planning portal (https://development.towerhamlets.gov.uk/online-applications/) carries the full application history.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Wood Wharf is **ascending through 2027**, and the office-to-residential conversion pipeline extends the ascending runway beyond that into the 2030s. Specifically: each new Wood Wharf residential tower delivered between now and August 2027 adds real daily-life density (more people means more retail activation, more restaurants opening, more weekend activity, which is the thing Wood Wharf has been working toward from a low base). The Canary Wharf office-to-resi conversion story is the genuine upside bet — if even one of the proposed repurposings delivers inside the window, it transforms the financial core's day/night rhythm problem materially. Even if none of them delivers, the Wood Wharf side alone keeps the ascending trajectory live.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **strong pass** — active Wood Wharf delivery, office-to-resi conversions in flight, multiple cranes. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **strong pass** — ascending through 2027, with additional office-conversion upside. Confidence: high.
- Category: **ascending through 2027**, with upside from office-to-resi conversion pipeline.

### elephant-park — Elephant Park

#### Current status
Lendlease's Elephant Park (https://www.elephantpark.co.uk/) is in its final delivery phase. The masterplan replaced the Heygate Estate with ~3,000 homes across multiple plots plus the Elephant Park central green space, Sayer Street retail, and the Castle Square food/retail cluster. The Southwark planning portal (https://planning.southwark.gov.uk/online-applications/) records the delivered phases. The site is ~85% complete with only a couple of plots outstanding at the southern and eastern edges. Palace skatepark has been retained and integrated into the public realm; the new Northern Line exit at Elephant & Castle is operating.

#### Pipeline
The final plots of Elephant Park are scheduled for completion in 2026-2027. Separately but adjacently, Delancey's Elephant & Castle Town Centre redevelopment (replacing the old shopping centre site) is under delivery by Get Living and partners, with completion of the first residential towers expected in 2025-2026 and further phases running into 2027-2028. The distinction matters: the Lendlease masterplan is almost done, but the Delancey/Get Living masterplan next door is only ~25% through its delivery, which keeps the wider Elephant & Castle area ascending even as Elephant Park specifically winds down.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Elephant Park itself is **peaking 2025-2026**, but the wider **Elephant & Castle** area (Elephant Park + Elephant & Castle Town Centre + UAL LCC) is **ascending through 2027** on the back of the Delancey delivery and the UAL rebuild. Caner's lens is area-level not project-level, so the honest trajectory call is ascending into 2027. By August 2027 the town centre redevelopment will have added substantial new residential, retail, and public realm on top of the already-complete Elephant Park, with more still under construction into 2028.

#### T3.1 / T5.3 call
- **T3.1**: **pass** — Lendlease long tail plus Delancey in active delivery. Confidence: high.
- **T5.3**: **pass** — ascending via the Delancey/UAL side even as Elephant Park specifically winds down. Confidence: high.
- Category: **ascending through 2027** (area-level), noting the Lendlease half is peaking while the Delancey half is ascending.

### royal-wharf-royal-docks — Royal Wharf & Royal Docks

#### Current status

### old-oak-common — Old Oak Common

#### Current status

The Old Oak and Park Royal Development Corporation (OPDC, https://www.london.gov.uk/programmes-strategies/planning/implementing-london-plan/opportunity-areas/old-oak-and-park-royal) is the GLA-run delivery vehicle. The HS2 Old Oak Common station (https://www.hs2.org.uk/where/stations/old-oak-common/) is under construction with a delivery window that has slipped multiple times — the current realistic opening is 2030+, depending on HS2 Phase 1 progress. The station is the entire regeneration thesis: without it, Old Oak Common has no transport, no jobs, no anchors, and no residential demand.

#### Pipeline
OPDC has outline plans for ~25,000 homes and major commercial floorspace across the opportunity area. Delivery is contingent on station opening. First residential phases by various developers (Genesis, Queen's Park Rangers' stadium-adjacent proposals, etc.) have appeared in planning but are early-stage.

#### Trajectory through 2027
Old Oak Common is **ascending through 2027 but from a very low base**. The station is under construction, visible enabling works are happening, and the area is closer to reality in August 2027 than in April 2026. But the actual residential / neighbourhood proposition is outside the window — it is a 2030s+ story. For Caner specifically, Old Oak Common as a *place to live today* is essentially a non-starter; as a *2030s bet* it is interesting; as an August 2027 target it is too early.

## Cross-cutting findings

### Operator-level patterns

**Berkeley Group (including St James, St George, St William)**. Berkeley runs the single largest portfolio of active London masterplans in the dataset. Across Kidbrooke Village, Woodberry Down, Bromley-by-Bow, Oval Village, Southall Waterside, Royal Arsenal Riverside, White City Living, Filmworks, and others, Berkeley's delivery rhythm is consistent: long horizons (10+ years), phased delivery tightly tied to sales velocity, patient capital, and a willingness to hold consented land across cycles. Every Berkeley-run masterplan in the dataset that is earlier than 50% complete as of 2026 is effectively guaranteed to be ascending through August 2027. The Berkeley pattern is the single best "is this area ascending" heuristic in the dataset. The weakness is that the ascent is shallow — Berkeley masterplans add housing steadily rather than transforming places overnight.

**Argent / Related Argent**. King's Cross is the reference plateau; Brent Cross Town is the reference ascending case. Roger Madelin moved from Argent to British Land and is now running Canada Water. The Argent lineage is therefore present in three of the strongest cases in the dataset. Canada Water inherits King's Cross's delivery discipline and is the single cleanest "credible masterplan in early stage" case.

**Lendlease**. Elephant Park, International Quarter London at Stratford, and Silvertown Quays. Lendlease's post-Elephant-Park portfolio is Stratford commercial and Silvertown. Lendlease delivery quality is good but schedule adherence is mixed — Silvertown has slipped years behind the original plan. The Lendlease pattern therefore splits: the completed schemes are strong plateaus, the active schemes carry real slippage risk.

**Quintain**. Single-site specialist at Wembley Park. Quintain's model is closest to Get Living's East Village — a single-operator campus run as an ongoing BTR business. The Wembley Park pattern is unique in the dataset and does not generalise to other areas.

**Ballymore (including EcoWorld Ballymore)**. Wood Wharf (part-owner), Embassy Gardens, Royal Wharf, London City Island, West Hampstead Square, Goodluck Hope, Leamouth. Ballymore's pattern is waterside-led placemaking. Most of the Ballymore portfolio in the dataset is in the peaking / plateauing band, with Wood Wharf as the main ascending exception (Wood Wharf ascends because Canary Wharf Group co-owns and drives delivery).

**Knight Dragon**. Single-site specialist at Greenwich Peninsula. Re-phased multiple times. Still actively delivering but at a slower cadence than originally planned. Ascending but with slippage risk.

**British Land**. Canada Water is the flagship — early-stage, Madelin-led, highest credibility. Paddington Central, Broadgate (outside the Phase F scope, but worth noting as the office-to-resi conversion bellwether), and other British Land sites are plateaued in this dataset.

**Related Argent**. Brent Cross Town. Early-stage, Argent-lineage, highest credibility on discipline. Flagship ascending case.

**Delancey / LCR / Get Living (interlinked)**. King's Cross ownership-era, East Village operator, Elephant & Castle Town Centre, Earl's Court joint venture. Very mixed portfolio — some plateau (East Village), some peaking (E&C Town Centre first phases), some ascending flagship (Earl's Court). The Delancey relationship with Qatari capital is the main sensitivity — financial restructuring has affected several schemes historically.

### Borough-level patterns

**Tower Hamlets**. Has the highest density of small-to-medium residential permissions in the dataset. Accretional gentrification rather than masterplan-led. Borough-wide trajectory is positive through 2027 via Canary Wharf Group's office-to-resi pipeline, Wood Wharf delivery, Whitechapel Elizabeth Line effect, and continuous small-scale activity. LBTH as a whole is ascending through 2027.

**Newham**. Split between the ascending Stratford cluster (LLDC-driven), the ascending Silvertown / Royal Docks cluster (GLA-driven with Lendlease), and the slower Canning Town / Plaistow / West Ham cluster. The borough as a whole is ascending through 2027 but the ascent is highly uneven across postcodes.

**Southwark**. Strongest operator lineup of any borough — British Land at Canada Water, Delancey at Elephant & Castle, NHG/Southwark Council at Aylesbury, Grosvenor at Biscuit Factory, Lendlease at Elephant Park. Southwark is the single best borough for "masterplan ascending through 2027" density.

**Barnet**. Dominated by Brent Cross Town. Outside Brent Cross Town, Barnet is mostly plateaued.

**Wandsworth**. Battersea Power Station (peaking), Nine Elms portion (peaking), Ram Quarter (peaking), Wandsworth Riverside (mature). Wandsworth was the headline ascending borough of 2018-2023 and is now transitioning to plateau.

**Haringey**. Tottenham Hale is the flagship; Wood Green is stalled; Woodberry Down (partly Hackney) is ascending. Haringey's ascending-through-2027 story is concentrated in Tottenham Hale.

**Greenwich**. Greenwich Peninsula, Woolwich (Royal Arsenal), Kidbrooke Village, Charlton Riverside. Strong borough for ascending cases across multiple sites, with schedule slippage being the common risk.

**Brent**. Wembley Park (peaking), Brent Cross Town southern edge (ascending). Concentrated around two big sites; the rest of the borough is plateaued.

**Hackney**. Woodberry Down is the major ascending case. Hackney Central and Hackney Wick are ascending slowly. Core gentrified areas (Dalston, Stoke Newington, Highbury) are plateaued.

**Camden**. King's Cross is the reference plateau; Hawley Wharf is plateaued; Kentish Town station reopens inside the window; the rest of the borough is mature and plateaued. Camden's ascending story is concentrated in the Euston HS2 area, which is partly outside the scope of this focus because HS2 Phase 1 delivery is 2030+.

**Ealing**. North Acton (ascending aggressively), Ealing Broadway (peaking), Southall (ascending), Acton (ascending slowly), Park Royal (OPDC long-horizon). Ealing is one of the stronger ascending boroughs after Southwark.

### Schedule slippage pattern

Across the dataset, the London Plan opportunity areas consistently run 3–5 years behind their adopted timetables. Specific examples:

- **Royal Docks / Silvertown**: originally 2025-heavy delivery, now 2030s-heavy.
- **Greenwich Peninsula**: originally a 2020s masterplan, now a 2030s masterplan.
- **Old Oak Common**: HS2 station originally targeted for 2026, now 2030+.
- **Nine Elms**: delivered close to original schedule — the exception rather than the rule.
- **Battersea Power Station**: delivered close to original schedule for Phases 1-3, slipping on Phase 4+.
- **Canada Water**: too early in delivery to assess slippage; Madelin lineage suggests discipline.
- **Brent Cross Town**: enabling infrastructure (station) delivered on time; residential phases running close to plan.
- **Elephant Park**: Lendlease close to plan.
- **Earl's Court**: early stage; the outline-consent-to-first-delivery transition is the slippage-risk moment.

The implication for the 2027 horizon is that any area whose claim to T5.3 rests on phases scheduled for exactly 2027 should be treated as phases likely to deliver in 2028-2029. Areas whose T5.3 claim rests on multiple phases across 2026-2030 are more robust because some slippage is absorbed by the pipeline depth.

1. **Silvertown Quays delivery schedule**. Lendlease publicly re-phased Silvertown in 2024-2025 and the current 2026-2027 delivery window depends on the revised programme holding. If Silvertown slips further, Royal Docks' ascending-through-2027 call weakens to peaking-later. Verification path: Newham planning portal + Lendlease investor updates + NLA tracker.

2. **BPSDC financial position and Phase 4+ delivery**. Battersea Power Station's later-phase viability has been repeatedly questioned in the property press. If BPSDC restructures or pauses later phases, the peaking call on BPS becomes a plateaued-or-declining call. Verification path: Wandsworth planning portal + BPSDC investor communications + EG / FT coverage.

3. **Aylesham Centre / Peckham**. The Berkeley Aylesham proposal could transform Peckham's trajectory if it achieves consent and starts on site inside the 2026-2027 window. Current status is contested. Verification path: Southwark planning portal + community forum coverage + Berkeley scheme pages.

4. **Earl's Court outline consent**. The 2025 outline consent milestone is the single most important pre-delivery event in the west London pipeline. Verification path: ECDC updates + LBHF + RBKC planning portals + NLA tracker.

5. **Old Oak Common HS2 station opening**. Current "mid-2030s" realistic window, but HS2 Phase 1 schedule continues to drift. Verification path: HS2 official updates + Network Rail bulletins + DfT announcements.

6. **Canada Water A1 plot delivery date**. British Land has published a target for the first residential plot delivery but exact completion dates for inside-the-window delivery are not fully specified in public documents. 7. **Brent Cross Town retail activation**. The first town-centre retail is scheduled for inside the 2027 window. Whether activation is "soft-launch" or "fully-programmed" by August 2027 is unclear from public sources. Verification path: Related Argent updates + Barnet planning portal.

8. **Convoys Wharf first residential phase**. Delivery has been repeatedly delayed. Whether any occupied residential is in place by August 2027 is genuinely uncertain. Verification path: Lewisham planning portal + Convoys Wharf marketing updates.
